## Why Karangan Matters

Paper 1 carries 40 marks (20% of total). While it is not the highest-weighted component, it is often the most memorable part of the exam — and students who write well consistently pull up their overall grade. More importantly, for the interactive digital practice tool, karangan practice is highly gamifiable.

**Key challenge:** Students communicate in English at home and school. Limited Malay vocabulary makes it hard to describe emotions, characters, and settings naturally. Consistent weekly practice is the single most effective solution.

## The Two Question Types

### Type 1: Topic-Based Composition (Karangan Bertajuk)

Students write freely based on a given topic. No pictures.

**Common PSLE topics (recurring themes):**
- Hari yang tidak terlupakan (An unforgettable day)
- Pertolongan (Helping someone)
- Kejadian di pantai / taman / sekolah (Incident at the beach/park/school)
- Cita-cita saya (My ambition)
- Hari jadi yang istimewa (A special birthday)
- Menghadapi cabaran (Facing a challenge)
- Kawan baik (A good friend)
- Kemalangan (An accident)
- Berlaku jujur (Being honest)
- Pengalaman pertama (A first experience)

**Students must independently create:**
- Setting (time and place)
- Characters (names, descriptions)
- Problem/conflict
- Resolution
- Lesson/moral

---

### Type 2: Picture-Based Composition (Karangan Bergambar)

Students write based on 3–4 pictures showing a sequence of events.

**What pictures provide:** A plot framework
**What students must add:** Dialogue, emotions, descriptions, smooth transitions, beginning before picture 1, and ending after picture 4

**Important:** One paragraph per picture. Each paragraph minimum 3–4 sentences.

## The 7-Step Writing Process

### Step 1: Choose Your Question Wisely (5 minutes)
- Read BOTH questions before deciding
- Choose the one where you can picture the story clearly
- For picture-based: make sure you understand the sequence
- Don't choose what looks "easier" — choose what you can write well

### Step 2: Plan Your Composition (5 minutes)

**For topic-based, jot down:**
- Who (characters + names)
- Where + When (setting)
- What problem occurs
- How it is resolved
- Lesson learned

**For picture-based, write 1–2 notes beside each picture:**
- What is happening
- What will the characters say/feel

**Time investment:** 5 minutes of planning saves 15 minutes of writing confusion

### Step 3: Write a Strong Introduction (10 minutes)

**Avoid generic openers:**
- ❌ "Pada suatu hari..."
- ❌ "Pagi itu adalah hari biasa..."

**Use specific, atmospheric openers:**
- ✅ "Pagi Sabtu yang cerah itu, adik saya dan saya menuju ke pantai..."
- ✅ "Bunyi loceng sekolah bergema menandakan waktu rehat telah tiba..."
- ✅ "Langit mendung dan angin bertiup kencang ketika kami tiba di padang..."

**Good introduction includes:**
- Setting (place + time) with description
- Character introduction
- Hint of what is about to happen (mood/foreshadowing)

### Step 4: Develop the Plot with Rich Details (20 minutes)

Each middle paragraph should:
- Begin a new scene or event
- Have one main idea
- Include at least 3–4 sentences
- Add sensory details (sight, sound, feeling)
- Include dialogue (but balanced — not a drama script)

**Details to include:**
- Weather and environment: *Matahari bersinar terik, membakar kulit kami.*
- Character feelings: *Hatiku berdegup kencang ketika aku melihat...*
- Physical descriptions: *Seorang lelaki tua berambut putih dan berjalan dengan tongkat...*
- Dialogue: adds life and character

### Step 5: Use Proverbs Accurately (within Step 4 or 5)

Use 1 peribahasa, naturally embedded in the narrative. Only use it if you are sure of the meaning and context. See the Peribahasa Reference document for a full list.

**Example:**
> *Ahmad sentiasa membantu Razif, sahabatnya sejak kecil. Seperti kata peribahasa, "seperti isi dengan kuku", mereka tidak pernah berpisah.*

### Step 6: Write an Effective Conclusion (8 minutes)

**Avoid cliché endings:**
- ❌ "Itulah kejadian yang tidak dapat saya lupakan."
- ❌ "Sejak hari itu, saya tidak akan mengulangi kesilapan itu lagi."

**Show growth, reflection, or learning through action/thought:**
- ✅ *Sambil mengemas barang pulang, saya terasa betapa pentingnya persahabatan yang tulen.*
- ✅ *Senyuman ibu itu sudah cukup membuat hatiku puas, walaupun badan masih terasa penat.*

**Conclusion should:**
- Resolve the conflict satisfactorily
- Show character change or understanding
- State a lesson or moral naturally (not forcefully)

### Step 7: Check Your Composition (2 minutes)
- Scan for obvious spelling errors
- Check affix usage (me-, ber-, -kan, -an)
- Check punctuation (dialogue format)
- Ensure minimum 150 words
- Do NOT rewrite — only make small corrections

### Dialogue Verbs (Kata-kata untuk Dialog)

Instead of always using *berkata*, vary with:

| Situation | Malay verb |
|---|---|
| Said | berkata, menyebut |
| Asked | bertanya, meminta |
| Answered | menjawab, membalas |
| Shouted | menjerit, berteriak |
| Whispered | berbisik |
| Complained | merungut, mengadu |
| Laughed | ketawa, tergelak |
| Cried | menangis, meraung |
| Sighed | mengeluh, mengucapkan |
| Warned | memberi amaran |

## Common Student Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Weak/illogical plot | Plan before writing (Step 2) |
| Repeated vocabulary (e.g., only "berkata") | Use vocabulary lists above |
| Missing/wrong affixes | Review Tatabahasa document |
| Dialogue without proper format | See format below |
| Composition too short (< 150 words) | Add descriptions and dialogue |
| Cliché opening ("Pada suatu hari...") | Use specific, atmospheric openers |
| Forcing in peribahasa | Only use 1, only if sure of context |

## Dialogue Format (Very Commonly Tested)

**Correct Format:**

```
"Tolong saya!" jerit Aishah dengan suara yang lantang.
Ahmad berkata, "Jangan risau, saya akan membantu kamu."
"Terima kasih banyak-banyak," kata Aishah sambil menghela nafas lega.
```

**Rules:**
- Opening quotation mark before first word of speech
- Closing quotation mark AFTER punctuation (comma or full stop)
- Comma before quotation mark if explanatory phrase follows
- Full stop or exclamation mark if explanatory phrase precedes
- New paragraph for each new speaker

**Common error:**
- ❌ Ahmad berkata "Jangan risau" (missing comma and quotation marks)
- ❌ "Terima kasih." kata Aishah (punctuation outside quotes wrong)

## Practice Schedule (For PSLE 2026)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| Now–June 2026 | 1 composition per week (either type, alternating) |
| July–August 2026 | 2 compositions per week; timed (45 min + 5 min check) |
| September 2026 | 1 composition per week; focus on checking and polishing |

**How to practise effectively:**
1. Write under timed conditions (50 minutes maximum)
2. Use the 7-step process every time
3. Have an adult or teacher mark it with focus on: language, plot, vocabulary
4. Rewrite the same composition incorporating feedback
5. File all compositions — review progress monthly

## Model Composition Structure (Template)

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction (Setting + Characters + Mood)
[2–3 sentences describing place, time, characters, atmosphere]

PARAGRAPH 2 — Rising Action (Problem begins)
[3–4 sentences: what happened, initial reactions, dialogue]

PARAGRAPH 3 — Climax (Main event/conflict)
[4–5 sentences: most exciting/intense moment, emotions, details]
[Optional: insert peribahasa here naturally]

PARAGRAPH 4 — Falling Action (Resolution begins)
[3–4 sentences: how problem is addressed, who helps, what happens]

PARAGRAPH 5 — Conclusion (Lesson/Reflection)
[2–3 sentences: character reflects, lesson learned, looking forward]
```
