## Overview

Paper 2 Booklet B has two components:
1. **Lengkapkan Dialog (Complete the Dialogue)** — 8 marks, fill in 4 blanks
2. **Kefahaman (Comprehension)** — 32 marks, MCQ + open-ended questions

Total Paper 2 Booklet B: **40 marks**. This is your biggest single source of marks after Booklet A tatabahasa.

**No dictionary allowed for Paper 2.**

---

## COMPONENT 1: Lengkapkan Dialog (8 marks)

### What it involves:
- A short dialogue between 2–3 characters
- 4 blanks to fill in (2 marks each)
- Blanks test your understanding of natural conversation flow, vocabulary, and grammar

### Strategies:

1. **Read the full dialogue first** before attempting any blank
2. **Understand the context** — Who are the speakers? What are they talking about? What is the tone?
3. **Look at what comes before AND after the blank** — both are clues
4. **The blank must be grammatically correct AND make sense in context**
5. **Keep answers concise** — full sentences are not always required; match the conversational register

### Types of blanks:
- Missing response to a question
- Missing question for a given response
- Missing expression of emotion/reaction
- Missing information that logically connects two statements

### Example:

> **Ali:** "Ke mana kamu hendak pergi, Siti?"
> **Siti:** "_____________" (Blank 1)
> **Ali:** "Boleh saya ikut sama?"
> **Siti:** "Boleh. Tapi kamu mesti bersiap segera kerana kami bertolak dalam sepuluh minit lagi."

*Blank 1 answer:* "Saya hendak ke pasar raya bersama ibu saya." (or similar response answering where she's going)

## COMPONENT 2: Kefahaman (Comprehension — 32 marks)

### Text Types in PSLE Comprehension

| Type | Ciri-ciri | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Naratif (Narrative) | Story with characters, events, plot | Short stories, personal accounts |
| Ekspositori (Expository) | Facts and information | Articles about nature, Singapore, science |
| Prosedural (Procedural) | Steps and instructions | Recipes, how-to guides |
| Persuasif (Persuasive) | Arguments and opinions | Letters, opinion pieces |

---

### Question Types

**1. Literal Questions (Tersurat)**
- Answer is directly stated in the text
- Strategy: Find the exact sentence in the passage; paraphrase in your own words

**2. Inferential Questions (Tersirat)**
- Answer requires reading between the lines
- Strategy: Use clues in the text + logical reasoning; never guess wildly

**3. Vocabulary-in-Context Questions**
- Ask for the meaning of a word/phrase as used in the passage
- Strategy: Look at surrounding sentences for context clues; choose meaning that fits the passage, not the general definition

**4. Language Use Questions**
- Ask why the writer used a particular word/phrase/technique
- Strategy: Think about the effect created (emphasise? create mood? show contrast?)

---

## 5-Step Answering Framework (TRACE)

**T — Text type first.** Identify whether the passage is narrative, expository, persuasive, or procedural. This tells you what kind of language and content to expect.

**R — Read the questions BEFORE reading the passage.** Know what you're looking for before you start reading. Underline key words in each question.

**A — Active reading.** As you read the passage, mark/underline relevant sections for each question. Use pencil.

**C — Close reading for inference.** For "tersirat" questions, look for word choices, tone, and context clues.

**E — Evidence first.** Every answer should be anchored in the text. Quote or paraphrase from the passage before giving your explanation.

## How to Answer Open-Ended Comprehension Questions

### Format: Quote → Link → Explain

**Step 1 — Find the relevant part of the passage**
**Step 2 — Quote or paraphrase it in your answer**
**Step 3 — Link to what the question is asking**
**Step 4 — Explain why/how**

**Example Question:**
*"Apakah yang menunjukkan bahawa Ahmad adalah seorang pelajar yang rajin?"*

**Weak answer:** "Ahmad belajar setiap hari."

**Strong answer:** "Ahmad sanggup belajar sehingga larut malam walaupun badannya terasa penat. Hal ini menunjukkan bahawa dia sangat bersungguh-sungguh dalam pelajarannya dan tidak mudah berputus asa."

**Why the strong answer works:**
- Uses evidence from text ("belajar sehingga larut malam")
- Adds explanation of WHY this shows he is hardworking
- Uses richer vocabulary (bersungguh-sungguh, tidak mudah berputus asa)

## Vocabulary Strategies for Comprehension

### How to answer vocabulary questions:

**Q: Apakah maksud perkataan "terkejut" dalam ayat 5?**

**Method:**
1. Read sentences BEFORE and AFTER the word in context
2. Ask: What happened just before? How did the character react?
3. Give a definition that fits the context, NOT just a dictionary definition
4. If asked for a synonym, give a word that can replace it in the sentence

**Common vocabulary tested (and synonyms):**

| Word | Meaning / Synonyms |
|---|---|
| tergesa-gesa | in a hurry, rushing — bergegas, terburu-buru |
| mencegah | to prevent — menghalang, menghentikan |
| berbangga | proud — megah, bangga |
| deria | senses — perasaan deria |
| membantutkan | to stifle/hinder — menghalang, merencat |
| menangis teresak-esak | to sob — menangis dengan kuat, meraung |
| ternganga | mouth agape — terbuka mulut terkejut |
| menghampiri | to approach — mendekati, menuju ke arah |
| berkerut | frowning/wrinkled — mengerutkan dahi |
| terharu | moved/touched emotionally — tersentuh hati |

## Common Comprehension Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---|---|
| Copying whole paragraphs | Extract only the relevant part |
| Answering in English | Always answer in Malay |
| Incomplete answers | Always explain — quote + link + explain |
| Wrong tense in answers | Match the tense of the question and passage |
| Not using passage evidence | Ground every answer in the text |
| Answering what you think, not what the text says | For literal questions, stay true to the text |

## Inference Practice — Sample Questions

**Passage excerpt:**
*"Meskipun hujan turun dengan lebat, Puan Rashidah tetap keluar dari rumahnya. Dia memegang payungnya erat-erat dan melangkah ke kedai ubat yang terletak di hujung jalan."*

**Question 1 (literal):** Di manakah Puan Rashidah menuju?
**Answer:** Puan Rashidah menuju ke kedai ubat yang terletak di hujung jalan.

**Question 2 (inference):** Apakah yang dapat kamu rumuskan tentang kepentingan perjalanan Puan Rashidah?
**Answer:** Perjalanan Puan Rashidah adalah sangat penting kerana beliau sanggup keluar walaupun dalam keadaan hujan lebat. Ini menunjukkan bahawa keperluan atau urusan beliau tidak boleh ditangguhkan.

**Question 3 (vocabulary):** Apakah maksud "erat-erat" dalam konteks petikan itu?
**Answer:** "Erat-erat" bermaksud dengan kuat dan teguh. Dalam konteks ini, Puan Rashidah memegang payungnya dengan kuat supaya payung itu tidak terbang ditiup angin ketika hujan.
