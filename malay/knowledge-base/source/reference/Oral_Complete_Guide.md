# PSLE Malay Language — Oral Communication Guide (Lisan)
> Paper 4 | 50 marks | 25% weightage | ~10 minutes
> Held separately in mid-August 2026
> For P6 PSLE 2026 preparation

---

## Why Oral Matters So Much

**25% of total marks** — making it the second-highest weighted component after Paper 2. Many students neglect oral preparation because it feels less "studyable" than grammar or comprehension. But this is a mistake: oral is highly trainable with consistent practice.

The challenge for many Malay-speaking families in Singapore: English dominates daily conversation. Students may understand Malay but speak haltingly, use simple vocabulary, or run out of things to say under exam pressure.

## Format of PSLE Malay Oral (Paper 4)

| Component | Description | Duration (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| Reading Aloud | Student reads a passage to the examiner | 2–3 minutes |
| Stimulus-Based Conversation | Student watches a video clip, then answers prompts | 5–7 minutes |
| **Total** | | ~10 minutes |

---

## COMPONENT 1: Reading Aloud (Membaca Kuat)

### What is tested:
- Pronunciation (sebutan) — correct Malay phonetics
- Fluency (kelancaran) — no long pauses or stumbling
- Expression (ekspresi) — appropriate intonation, not monotone
- Accuracy — reading what is written, not adding or omitting words

### Marking Criteria
| Criterion | What Examiners Look For |
|---|---|
| Pronunciation | Clear, accurate Malay sounds; correct stress |
| Fluency | Smooth reading without excessive hesitation |
| Expression | Appropriate tone for content (e.g., sad passage = softer tone) |
| Accuracy | Not skipping words, not changing words |

### Preparation Strategies

**Daily reading practice (5–10 minutes per day):**
- Read Malay storybooks aloud
- Read Berita Harian (Malay newspaper) articles aloud
- Practice reading past oral passages from Mendaki or school materials

**Specific techniques:**
1. Pause at commas (,) and full stops (.)
2. Stress content words (nouns, verbs, key adjectives)
3. Read at a moderate pace — not too fast (rushing = mistakes), not too slow (sounds unnatural)
4. Practice with a parent/sibling listening — get feedback on unclear words
5. Record yourself on phone → listen back for pronunciation issues

**Common pronunciation challenges for Singapore Malay students:**

| Sound | Common Error | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| 'r' (rolled) | Softened/dropped | Should have slight roll: *rasa, rumah* |
| Final consonants | Often softened | Enunciate clearly: *tidak, sebab, hendak* |
| ng vs. n | Confused | *nyanyi* (ny), *nganga* (ng) |
| 'e' (two sounds) | Confused | *e pepet* (tengah) vs *e taling* (kena) |
| Long vowels | Shortened | *besar* (2 distinct syllables) |

## COMPONENT 2: Stimulus-Based Conversation (Perbualan Berasaskan Rangsangan)

### What happens:
1. Student watches a **short video clip** (about 1–2 minutes long)
2. Examiner asks **prompt questions** based on the video
3. Student must **extend responses** with opinions, explanations, examples, experiences, suggestions

### What is tested:
- Ability to describe what happened in the video
- Ability to give opinions and justify them
- Vocabulary and sentence variety
- Ability to relate to personal experience
- Fluency and naturalness of Malay speech

### The OREO Framework for Oral Responses

Use OREO to structure every answer:

| Letter | Meaning | What to say |
|---|---|---|
| **O** | Opinion | State your view clearly |
| **R** | Reason | Give at least 1–2 reasons |
| **E** | Example | Support with specific example (personal or observed) |
| **O** | Opinion (restate) | Conclude by restating your view |

**Example Question:** *"Adakah kamu bersetuju bahawa anak-anak harus membantu kerja rumah?"*

**OREO Response:**
> "Saya bersetuju bahawa anak-anak seharusnya membantu kerja rumah. *(Opinion)*
> Ini kerana apabila anak-anak membantu, mereka belajar untuk bertanggungjawab dan tidak bergantung kepada ibu bapa sahaja. Selain itu, ibu bapa pasti berasa gembira dan tidak terlalu penat setelah pulang bekerja. *(Reason)*
> Contohnya, saya selalu membantu ibu saya mengemas dapur setiap petang. Ibu saya berasa sangat terharu dan sering memuji usaha saya. *(Example)*
> Oleh itu, saya rasa setiap anak perlu membantu kerja rumah mengikut kemampuan masing-masing." *(Opinion restated)*

### Useful Sentence Starters for Oral

**To give opinion:**
- Pada pendapat saya...
- Saya rasa / Saya berpendapat bahawa...
- Menurut saya...
- Saya bersetuju / tidak bersetuju kerana...

**To give reasons:**
- Ini kerana...
- Sebabnya ialah...
- Hal ini disebabkan oleh...
- Antara sebabnya ialah...

**To give examples:**
- Contohnya...
- Sebagai contoh...
- Misalnya...
- Pengalaman saya sendiri menunjukkan bahawa...

**To explain further:**
- Selain itu...
- Tambahan pula...
- Di samping itu...
- Malah...

**To conclude:**
- Oleh itu...
- Oleh sebab itu...
- Kesimpulannya...
- Dengan itu...

### Common Oral Mistakes and Fixes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| One-word answers ("Ya" / "Tidak") | Always expand — use OREO framework |
| Mixing English words | Build Malay vocabulary for common topics |
| Speaking too softly | Project voice; examiner must hear clearly |
| Long pauses / "umm" | Practise fluency; use filler phrases: *"Hmm, pada pendapat saya..."* |
| Only answering what was asked | Volunteer related opinions — show language range |
| Same sentence structure throughout | Vary with questions, exclamations, conditionals |

## Practice Plan for Oral

| Activity | Frequency | How |
|---|---|---|
| Read Malay text aloud | Daily (5 min) | Berita Harian, storybooks, old oral passages |
| Conversation in Malay at home | Daily (meal times) | Any topic — make it fun, not a test |
| Watch Suria Channel / Malay YouTube | 2–3× per week | Expose ear to natural Malay speech |
| Practice oral prompts | 2× per week | Use OREO; record on phone; review |
| Full mock oral session | Once per month | Parent/sibling plays examiner role |

**For Harman (parent who is not a Malay teacher):**
Even if your Malay is not strong, you can still:
1. Play examiner by asking simple questions from the OREO framework above
2. Focus on *fluency* — is the child speaking in complete sentences?
3. Record sessions and replay — the child can self-correct
4. Use Suria channel clips as practice stimuli — ask your son to describe what he sees

## Sample Oral Practice Questions by Theme

**Environment:**
- Apakah yang boleh kamu lakukan untuk menjaga kebersihan kawasan kamu?
- Mengapa sesetengah orang tidak mahu mengitar semula?
- Apakah akibatnya jika kita tidak menjaga alam sekitar?

**Family & Relationships:**
- Bagaimanakah cara kamu menunjukkan rasa sayang kepada keluarga?
- Mengapa penting untuk menghabiskan masa bersama-sama keluarga?
- Apakah pengalaman kamu membantu ibu bapa di rumah?

**Health:**
- Apakah aktiviti yang kamu suka lakukan untuk menjaga kesihatan?
- Mengapa ramai remaja hari ini kurang bersenam?
- Bagaimanakah kita boleh mengurangkan penggunaan telefon bimbit?

**Values:**
- Pernahkah kamu menghadapi situasi di mana kamu terpaksa bersikap jujur? Ceritakan.
- Mengapa tanggungjawab itu penting dalam kehidupan seharian?
- Apakah yang boleh kamu lakukan untuk membantu rakan yang sedang bermasalah?
