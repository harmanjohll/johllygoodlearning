How to use this

A short, honest guide for parents and students. 5 minutes to read.

What this is

A free, browser-based studio for a Primary 6 student in Singapore aiming for AL1 in PSLE English Language. It mirrors the existing Science Lab and Malay Studio. No login. No fees. No tracking. Everything stays in your browser.

Status today

This is the Phase 1 scaffold. The hub renders, the shared kernel is in place, three topic pages have Learn-tab content (Continuous Writing, Comprehension Open-Ended, Stimulus-Based Conversation). The remaining nine topic pages, the AI Coach modes, the Drill engine, the Mind Map editor, and the cross-page modes (Compass, Mirror, Review) land in later phases — per docs/english-architecture.md.

Set up (2 minutes)

  1. Click Settings in the top right of any page.
  2. Get a free Google AI Studio API key ↗. Sign in with any Google account; no credit card needed.
  3. Paste the key. Click Test Key, then Save Key.
  4. The same key unlocks AI Coach features in /lab/ and /malay/ too.

What the student sees

  • Hub — one card per topic, grouped by paper. Mastery % per card. Click any "ready" card.
  • Topic page — four tabs: Learn (lesson + glossary + PSLE tips), Drill, Quiz, Mind Map. Phase 1 ships Learn; the rest land in later phases.
  • Format — the canonical 200-mark, 4-paper breakdown.
  • Streak — shows on the hub. Open any topic once a day to keep it alive. Rest days are honoured.

The AL1 thesis

The gap between AL2 and AL1 in English is not about knowing more. It is about:

  1. Scientific language discipline. Fuzzy verbs (makes, gets, changes) become precise terms (photosynthesises, absorbs, condenses).
  2. Causal completeness in reasoning. AL3 says A and C; AL1 explains the mechanism B that connects them.
  3. Command-word literacy. Describe vs explain vs infer vs predict vs evaluate — each demands a different answer shape.
  4. Transfer. Novel passages don't rattle them, because they have internalised the principle, not the example.

Every feature in this studio (current and planned) serves one or more of these four.

Knowledge base

The studio is built on a curated knowledge base at english/knowledge-base/. Highlights:

  • Composition Writing Guide — Paper 1 priority reference, with Mentor exemplars section
  • Comprehension Strategy Guide — Paper 2 priority; RACE framework, all 5 components
  • Model Essays Annotated — three Band-5 mentor essays with paragraph-by-paragraph annotations
  • Show, Don't Tell — 70+ paired flat-vs-shown lines across 9 emotion categories
  • Idioms (expanded), Collocations, Connectors, Register, Visual Text examples — six standalone reference KBs

For parents

This studio is designed to support a learner, not to replace tutoring or classroom teaching. Best used in 20-minute sessions, not marathon evenings. Showing up daily beats catching up on weekends. Rest days are honoured.

Future phases add a Compass (session opener with mood check-in) and a Mirror (progress dashboard) plus an opt-in weekly parent digest. None of these phone home — everything is local.

Privacy

No accounts. No server. No telemetry. Your study data lives only in your browser's localStorage. Clear your browser data and it is gone — this is by design. Your Gemini API key is the only thing leaving your device, and only to Google (when you trigger an AI feature).

Trouble?

  • AI says "API key not set": click Settings, paste your key, click Save.
  • AI says "quota exceeded": Google's free tier has daily limits. Wait until tomorrow, or create a second key.
  • Topic card greyed out: that topic page lands in a later phase. Use a "ready" card for now.
  • Streak reset: the never-miss-twice rule kicks in if you skip two days running. The Compass (future phase) will offer rest days proactively.
