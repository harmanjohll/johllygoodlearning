## Overview

The PSLE English Language examination assesses students across four papers, each targeting a different language skill. The syllabus is designed by the Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board (SEAB) in collaboration with the Ministry of Education (MOE).

**Total marks: 200.** **Total time: about 4 hours 25 minutes** spread across four papers and (for Paper 4) individual oral sessions.

The 2025 syllabus revision (which applies to PSLE 2025, 2026, and later years until SEAB issues a further revision) introduced four notable changes:

1. **Paper 1** lost 5 marks overall (Continuous Writing −4, Situational Writing −1).
2. **Situational Writing** now includes a **critical-thinking content point** (one of the six bullet points is *not* in the stimulus; the student must generate it).
3. **Article writing** was added as a Situational Writing genre.
4. **Paper 4 (Oral)** gained 10 marks overall (Reading Aloud +5, SBC +5). A new **preamble** precedes the Reading Aloud passage. SBC stimuli switched from drawings/posters to **real-life photographs**. Reading Aloud and SBC are **no longer thematically linked**.

## Paper 1: Writing (50 marks | 1 hour 10 minutes)

### Purpose

Assesses the student's ability to write purposefully and accurately for different audiences and contexts.

### Part 1: Situational Writing (14 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Marks | 14 |
| Task | Write a functional piece based on a given situation |
| Word count | ~150 words (guideline, not strict) |
| Format types | Email, letter (formal/informal), report, speech, **article (new in 2025)**, notice, review, recount, diary entry |

#### What is tested

- **Task Fulfilment** — Did the student address all SIX content points? **One of the six is the "critical-thinking" / "expository" point**, and its information is **not** in the stimulus — the student must invent a specific, realistic detail.
- **Language & Organisation** — Grammar, vocabulary, format conventions (salutation, sign-off, heading), register matched to audience.

#### Marking Rubric (Situational Writing)

| Band | Task Fulfilment | Language & Organisation | Total |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Top band | All 6 points addressed; purpose, audience, context very clear | Accurate, varied; ideas well-linked and sequenced | 13–14 |
| Strong | 5 points addressed clearly | Mostly accurate | 10–12 |
| Pass | 4 points addressed; purpose partially clear | Some errors but meaning clear | 7–9 |
| Below pass | ≤3 points addressed | Frequent errors | Below 7 |

> **What examiners look for:** Every bullet point in the task must be addressed. Missing one bullet (especially the critical-thinking one) typically costs 2–3 marks. Register (formal vs informal) must match the situation and audience.

### Part 2: Continuous Writing (36 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Marks | 36 |
| Word count | At least 150 words; target 200–300 |
| Choices | 3 topic choices, each with a picture; student chooses 1 |
| Topic types | Narrative |

#### Sub-bands (placeholder — to be confirmed against the SEAB PDF)

| Area | Indicative Marks | What it Covers |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| Content | ~18 | Relevance, development, interest, sensory and emotional depth |
| Language & Organisation | ~18 | Vocabulary, sentence structure, grammar, paragraphing, transitions |

> The SEAB top-band descriptors require "fully relevant" content, "wide range of vocabulary and structures appropriately" used, and "very good sequencing, paragraphing, and linking of ideas".

#### What examiners reward in Continuous Writing

- Strong, specific openings (no "It was a bright sunny day…" generics).
- **Showing emotions through Face / Actions / Speech / Thoughts** (the F.A.S.T. lens) rather than telling them.
- A clear high-tension climax that drives a realisation, not just an event list.
- Reflective endings that show character growth, anchored in a specific image or action (not "I learnt a valuable lesson").

## Paper 2: Language Use & Comprehension (90 marks | 1 hour 50 minutes)

Paper 2 is divided into two booklets.

### Booklet A — MCQ (25 marks)

| Component | Items | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| Grammar MCQ | 10 × 1m | 10 |
| Vocabulary MCQ | 5 × 1m | 5 |
| Vocabulary Cloze | 5 × 1m | 5 |
| Visual Text Comprehension | 5 × 1m | 5 |
| **Booklet A total** | | **25** |

**Visual Text Comprehension** uses a poster, advertisement, infographic, brochure, or webpage screenshot. The five MCQs test literal recognition, inference, language use (why this word/phrase choice), and purpose.

### Booklet B — Open response (65 marks)

| Component | Items | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| Grammar Cloze | 10 × 1m | 10 |
| Editing for Grammar & Spelling | 10 × 1m (5 grammar + 5 spelling, indicative; verify against SEAB PDF) | 10 |
| Comprehension Cloze | 15 × 1m | 15 |
| **Synthesis & Transformation** | 5 × 2m | **10** |
| Comprehension Open-Ended | ~10 questions on one passage (narrative *or* non-narrative, 500–600 words) | 20 |
| **Booklet B total** | | **65** |

**Synthesis & Transformation** patterns commonly tested: conjunctions, relative clauses, if-conditionals, direct↔reported speech, active↔passive, inversion / emphasis structures.

**Comprehension Open-Ended** uses ONE passage. The genre rotates between years (narrative one year, non-narrative the next). Question types: literal, inferential, vocabulary-in-context, language use, author's purpose, evaluative.

**45 of the 90 marks** (Comprehension Cloze 15 + S&T 10 + Comprehension OE 20) are constructed-response — no options, no word bank.

## Paper 3: Listening Comprehension (20 marks | approximately 35 minutes)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Format | Audio recordings played by examiner; students answer on a separate answer sheet |
| Number of items | 20 |
| Marks | 1 mark each |
| Question types | MCQ |
| Text types heard | Conversations, announcements, instructions, narratives, short talks |

> Students cannot replay the audio. They listen once (sometimes twice for short clips). The paper is not available for review after audio ends.

## Paper 4: Oral Communication (40 marks | approximately 10 minutes per student)

### Reading Aloud (15 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Task | Read a ~150-word passage aloud to the examiner |
| New in 2025 | A short **preamble** precedes the passage, framing purpose / audience / context |
| Recommended approach | **PACT** (Purpose / Audience / Context / Tone) to interpret the preamble; **REAP** (Rhythm / Expression / Articulation / Pace) to deliver |
| Preparation | ~10 minutes |
| Marking strands | Pronunciation, fluency & pace, expression & stress (proportions adjusted upward from the pre-2025 10-mark version) |

### Stimulus-Based Conversation (25 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Task | Respond to questions about a **real-life photograph** (new in 2025; no longer drawings/posters) |
| Duration | ~5–7 minutes of conversation |
| Independence | Reading Aloud and SBC are **no longer thematically linked** (new in 2025) |
| Marking strands | Communication of ideas, vocabulary, language accuracy (proportions adjusted upward from the pre-2025 20-mark version) |
| Typical question arc | Q1 Observation + Inference → Q2 Personal Experience → Q3 Critical Opinion |
| Frameworks (per question type) | Q1: describe-then-infer; Q2: **PEEL** or **OREO**; Q3: **VBP** (Validate / Bridge / Persuade) |
| Themes covered | Everyday topics — environment, technology, school life, family, community, health |

## Marking Criteria Summary

### What earns full marks

| Paper | Key to full marks |
|-------|--------------------|
| Paper 1 Part 1 (Situational) | All 6 bullet points addressed (incl. the critical-thinking one); correct format conventions; register matched to audience |
| Paper 1 Part 2 (Continuous) | Engaging, well-paced narrative with sensory detail; varied vocabulary and sentence structure; reflective, image-anchored ending |
| Paper 2 OE Comprehension | RACE-structured answers (Restate, Answer, Cite, Explain); exact quotes for evidence; precise language-use explanations |
| Paper 2 Editing | Correct word identified AND correctly spelled correction |
| Paper 2 Synthesis & Transformation | Original meaning preserved; grammar perfect; no extraneous content added |
| Paper 3 | Active listening; prediction using question stems; process of elimination |
| Paper 4 Reading Aloud | Tone matched to preamble (PACT); clean articulation of "-ed", "-s", "-th" endings; varied pace |
| Paper 4 SBC | Extended responses with reasons and concrete examples; observe → infer pattern; balanced critical opinion (VBP) |
