# SEAB Official PSLE English Language Format & Syllabus 2026

---

## Overview

The PSLE English Language examination assesses students across four papers, each targeting a different language skill. The syllabus is designed by the Singapore Examinations and Assessment Board (SEAB) in collaboration with the Ministry of Education (MOE).

**Official Syllabus Reference:** EL (Primary) Syllabus 2024 onwards (applies to PSLE 2026)
**SEAB Website:** www.seab.gov.sg

---

## Paper 1: Writing (70 marks | 1 hour 50 minutes)

### Purpose
Assesses the student's ability to write purposefully and accurately for different audiences and contexts.

---

### Part 1: Situational Writing (30 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Marks | 30 |
| Task | Write a functional piece based on a given situation |
| Word count | ~120–150 words (guideline, not strict) |
| Format types | Email, letter (formal/informal), report, speech, notice, review, recount, diary entry |

#### What is tested:
- **Task achievement** — Did the student respond to all the bullet points given?
- **Language accuracy** — Is the grammar and vocabulary correct and appropriate?
- **Format and register** — Is the correct format used (e.g., salutation, sign-off, heading)?

#### Marking Rubric (Situational Writing):

| Band | Task Achievement | Language | Total |
|------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| Distinction | All points addressed fully; purpose clear | Accurate, varied language | 28–30 |
| Merit | Most points addressed; purpose generally clear | Mostly accurate | 23–27 |
| Pass | Some points addressed; purpose partially clear | Some errors | 17–22 |
| Below Pass | Few points addressed; purpose unclear | Frequent errors | Below 17 |

> **What examiners look for:** Every bullet point in the task must be addressed. Missing even one bullet point typically costs 3–5 marks. Register (formal vs informal) must match the situation.

---

### Part 2: Continuous Writing (40 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Marks | 40 |
| Word count | 150–300 words |
| Choices | 3 topic choices OR 1 picture prompt (student chooses 1) |
| Topic types | Narrative, personal recount, descriptive, reflective |

#### Sub-scores within 40 marks:

| Area | Marks | What It Covers |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Content | 20 | Ideas, relevance, development, interest |
| Language | 15 | Vocabulary, sentence structure, grammar, style |
| Organisation | 5 | Paragraphing, flow, opening and ending |

#### Content Bands:

| Band | Marks | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Band 1 | 17–20 | Engaging, well-developed, relevant ideas; vivid details |
| Band 2 | 13–16 | Generally relevant; ideas developed but may be uneven |
| Band 3 | 9–12 | Some relevant ideas; limited development |
| Band 4 | 5–8 | Ideas thin or off-topic; minimal development |
| Band 5 | 1–4 | Very little relevant content |

#### Language Bands:

| Band | Marks | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Band 1 | 13–15 | Wide range of vocabulary and structures; very few errors |
| Band 2 | 10–12 | Good range; some errors but meaning clear |
| Band 3 | 7–9 | Limited range; errors affect clarity occasionally |
| Band 4 | 4–6 | Very limited range; errors frequent |
| Band 5 | 1–3 | Extremely limited; errors throughout |

> **What examiners look for:** Strong openings and endings are noticed. Showing emotions (not just telling) earns more marks. Stories that go off-topic lose Content marks heavily. Aim for 200–250 words.

## Paper 2: Language Use & Comprehension (95 marks | 1 hour 50 minutes)

Paper 2 is divided into two booklets.

---

### Booklet A: Multiple Choice Questions (~28 marks)

#### Section 1: Grammar Cloze (~10 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Format | Passage with 10 blanks; 4 options (A/B/C/D) per blank |
| Tests | Tenses, subject-verb agreement, articles, prepositions, conjunctions, pronouns |
| Strategy | Read entire passage first; eliminate wrong options |

#### Section 2: Vocabulary MCQ (~18 marks)

| Sub-section | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Vocabulary Cloze | Passage with blanks; choose the best word from 4 options |
| Vocabulary (isolated) | Choose word closest in meaning to underlined word |
| Tests | Synonyms, antonyms, word meaning in context, collocation |

---

### Booklet B: Open-Ended Questions (~67 marks)

#### Component 1: Visual Text Comprehension (10 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Text type | Poster, advertisement, infographic, brochure, webpage screenshot |
| Question types | Literal (find the information), inferential (read between lines), language use (explain word/phrase choice) |
| Answer style | Short to medium answers; 1–3 sentences |

#### Component 2: Comprehension Cloze (15 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Format | Passage with 15 blanks; student supplies ONE word per blank |
| Tests | Grammar, vocabulary, contextual understanding, collocation |
| Marks | 1 mark per blank |

> Key rule: Only ONE word is accepted per blank. Contractions (e.g., "don't") count as ONE word. Hyphenated words are usually treated as one word.

#### Component 3: Editing for Spelling and Grammar (12 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Format | Passage with 12 errors; each line may have 0 or 1 error |
| Error types | Spelling (6 marks) + Grammar (6 marks) |
| Task | Identify the error word and write the correct word |
| Marks | 1 mark per correct identification and correction |

> Students must identify BOTH the wrong word AND write the correct form. Spelling errors and grammar errors are separate categories.

#### Component 4: Narrative Comprehension (15 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Passage type | Story or narrative extract (fiction or creative non-fiction) |
| Length | ~500–700 words |
| Question types | Literal (2m), inferential (2–3m), vocabulary-in-context (2m), language use (2m), author's purpose (2m) |
| Answer format | Full sentences expected for 2+ mark questions |

#### Component 5: Non-Narrative Comprehension (15 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Passage type | Informational, expository, or argumentative text |
| Length | ~500–700 words |
| Question types | Same spectrum as narrative — literal through to evaluative |
| Difference | May include questions on how information is organised, or on the author's argument |

---

## Paper 3: Listening Comprehension (20 marks | approximately 35 minutes)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Format | Audio recordings played by examiner; students answer on answer sheet |
| Number of items | 20 questions |
| Marks | 1 mark each |
| Question types | MCQ (mostly); some short-answer possible |
| Text types heard | Conversations, announcements, instructions, narratives, short talks |

> Students cannot replay the audio. They listen once (sometimes twice for short clips). The paper is not available for review after audio ends.

---

## Paper 4: Oral Communication (30 marks | approximately 10 minutes per student)

### Reading Aloud (10 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Task | Read a passage of ~150 words aloud to examiner |
| Preparation time | ~10 minutes to read and prepare |
| Marks | Pronunciation (4), Fluency & pace (3), Expression & stress (3) |

### Stimulus-Based Conversation (20 marks)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Task | Respond to questions about a picture or short video clip |
| Duration | ~5–7 minutes of conversation |
| Marks | Communication of ideas (8), Vocabulary (6), Language accuracy (6) |
| Topics covered | Everyday themes: environment, technology, school life, family, community |

---

## Marking Criteria Summary

### What Gets Full Marks

| Paper | Key to Full Marks |
|-------|------------------|
| Paper 1 Part 1 | All bullet points addressed; correct format; appropriate register |
| Paper 1 Part 2 | Engaging story; varied vocabulary; clear structure; 150-300 words |
| Paper 2 Comprehension | RACE-structured answers (Restate, Answer, Cite, Explain); exact quotes |
| Paper 2 Editing | Correct word identified AND correctly spelled correction |
| Paper 3 | Active listening; process of elimination |
| Paper 4 | Clear articulation; extended responses with reasons and examples |
