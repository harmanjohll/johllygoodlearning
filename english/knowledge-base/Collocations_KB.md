# Collocations Knowledge Base
### PSLE English Language — Primary 6

> **Status:** Authored 2026-05-13 from open dictionary sources (Oxford Collocations Dictionary online, Cambridge Dictionary, Just The Word). All items have been chosen for PSLE-level usefulness: they appear in Grammar Cloze, Vocabulary MCQ, Editing, Comprehension Cloze, and Composition vocabulary lift. ≥170 collocations total across 8 categories.

---

## What is a collocation?

A **collocation** is a pair (or group) of words that naturally go together. Native speakers expect *make a decision* (not *do a decision*); *heavy rain* (not *strong rain*); *fast food* (not *quick food*). Collocations are tested on every PSLE paper — Cloze and Vocab MCQ in Paper 2, register choices in Paper 1, and natural flow in Paper 4.

### Why collocations matter for AL1

- **Cloze accuracy.** ~3–4 of the 15 Comprehension Cloze blanks each year are decided by collocation knowledge alone.
- **Vocab MCQ.** ~1–2 of the 5 Vocab MCQ items test collocation directly ("which word fits with *the news…*?").
- **Composition lift.** Replacing *get a lot of money* with *make a great deal of money* signals AL1 register.
- **Editing.** Errors of the form *do a mistake* (not *make*) are common Editing traps.

## §8 — Singapore-specific collocations to use carefully

Some Singapore English collocations are NOT accepted as standard PSLE answers. Use the standard column.

| Singapore English | Standard (PSLE-safe) |
|---|---|
| revert (= reply) | reply / respond |
| open the light / close the light | turn on the light / turn off the light |
| chop (a document) | stamp (a document) |
| send my friend home | give my friend a lift home |
| can or not? | Is it possible? / Can you? |
| eat one's words (Singlish: = to be ashamed) | eat one's words (standard: = to admit you were wrong) |
| pass up (= submit) | hand in / submit |
| keep (= put away) | put away / store |

> **Editing trap:** these Singaporean uses are not "wrong" in conversation, but they cost marks in formal PSLE writing. The Editing component will sometimes flag *open the aircon* as a grammar/lexical error; the correction is *turn on the aircon* or *switch on the aircon*.

## Drills and practice patterns

For a tutoring app or workbook, the following item formats convert this KB into practice:

1. **Cloze with verb missing.** *She wanted to ______ a difference in her community.* (make)
2. **Choose-the-collocation MCQ.** *We had ______ rain last night.* (heavy / strong / hard / big)
3. **Spot the wrong collocation.** *He decided to do a mistake.* → *He decided to make a mistake.*
4. **Match the preposition.** *He apologised ___ being late.* (for)
5. **Replace the weak phrase.** *Get a lot of money* → *make a great deal of money / a fortune.*
