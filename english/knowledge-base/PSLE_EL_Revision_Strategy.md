# PSLE EL Revision Strategy
### Phase-by-Phase Plan: May 2026 to October 2026

---

## Overview

This guide provides a structured revision plan from now through the PSLE 2026 examinations in October. The plan is divided into three phases:

| Phase | Period | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Foundation | Now – June 2026 | Build core skills; identify and fix weaknesses |
| Intensive | July – August 2026 | Timed practice; exam conditioning |
| Polish | September – October 2026 | Full papers; refine weak areas; exam technique |

---

# PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Now – June 2026)

**Goal:** Build strong, consistent foundations in all components. No timed pressure yet — the focus is learning, not speed.

## Weekly Schedule (Foundation Phase)

| Day | Activity | Time | Focus |
|-----|---------|------|-------|
| Monday | Grammar drill | 20 min | 1 topic from Grammar_Complete_Reference |
| Tuesday | Vocabulary learning | 15 min | 5 new words from Vocabulary_Idioms_Bank |
| Wednesday | Comprehension practice | 30 min | 1 passage with RACE answers |
| Thursday | Writing practice | 30 min | 1 situational writing task |
| Friday | Oral practice | 15 min | Reading aloud + 5-min conversation with parent |
| Saturday | Review week's errors | 20 min | Go through mistakes; update error log |
| Sunday | Free reading | 20 min | Novel, magazine, or newspaper — any topic of interest |

**Total weekly time: approximately 2.5 hours**

## Foundation Phase Targets

| Component | Goal by End of June |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Grammar | Complete all 12 sections of Grammar_Complete_Reference |
| Comprehension | Use RACE correctly for every open-ended answer |
| Composition | Complete 4 compositions; reach Band 2 Content consistently |
| Vocabulary | Learn and actively use 60+ new words |
| Oral | Comfortable reading aloud; able to give PEEL responses |

## What to Do Each Week (Foundation)

### Weeks 1–2: Grammar Foundations
- Read: Tenses, Subject-Verb Agreement
- Practice: 10 grammar questions per day
- Quiz method: Harman reads a sentence aloud with an error; student corrects it

### Weeks 3–4: Writing Foundations
- Read: Composition_Writing_Guide — Story Arc, Showing vs Telling, Vocabulary Uplift
- Practice: Write one composition per week; focus on structure, not marks yet
- Harman marks for: Does it have a clear opening, climax, and ending?

### Weeks 5–6: Comprehension Foundations
- Read: Comprehension_Strategy_Guide — all question types
- Practice: 1 passage per week; answer all questions using RACE for 2+ marks
- Harman marks for: Did the student quote from the text?

### Weeks 7–8: Consolidation
- Mix of all components
- First attempt at a full Paper 1 (not timed) — mark it together
- First attempt at Paper 2 Booklet B (not timed) — mark it together

## Intensive Phase Targets

| Component | Goal by End of August |
|-----------|-----------------------|

| Composition | At least 2 compositions scoring Band 1 Content |

## How to Run a Timed Practice Session (Harman's Guide)

**For Paper 1 (Writing) — 50 marks, 1 hour 10 minutes total:**
1. Set a 25-minute timer for Part 1 (Situational Writing, 14 marks)
2. Set a 40-minute timer for Part 2 (Continuous Writing, 36 marks)
3. Leave ~5 minutes for checking
4. No dictionary allowed; no looking at notes
5. After: use the rubric in Composition_Writing_Guide to mark

**For Paper 2 (Language Use & Comprehension):**
1. Print a past year paper or use one from Popular
2. Time each section separately (see time allocations in Comprehension_Strategy_Guide)
3. After: go through answers question by question; discuss WHY wrong answers were wrong

# PHASE 3: POLISH (September – October 2026)

**Goal:** Perform at peak under exam conditions. No new learning — only refinement.

## Weekly Schedule (Polish Phase)

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|---------|------|
| Monday | Full Paper 2 under exam conditions | 110 min |
| Tuesday | Review Paper 2; drill 2–3 weak question types | 40 min |
| Wednesday | Full Paper 1 under exam conditions | 70 min |
| Thursday | Review Paper 1; rewrite weak composition paragraph | 40 min |
| Friday | Oral practice — full simulated oral | 15 min |
| Saturday | Rest / light review only | — |
| Sunday | Read an article aloud; 15 min vocabulary review | 20 min |

**Total weekly time: approximately 5 hours**

## Polish Phase Focus

1. **Prioritise weak areas from the error log** — not areas already strong
2. **Read exam instructions carefully every time** — many marks are lost to misreading
3. **Manage time strictly** — especially for Paper 2, which is dense
4. **Do not start any new topic after late September** — consolidate what you know

# HARMAN'S GUIDE: How to Help Without Being an English Teacher

This section is written for Harman specifically. You do not need to be an English expert to support your child's preparation.

## Your Three Roles

### Role 1: The Timekeeper
- Set timers strictly during practice
- Do not allow extra time beyond the paper's allocation
- Exam conditions mean: no phone, no dictionary, no help during the task

### Role 2: The Marker
- Use the rubrics in Guides 05 and 06 to mark comprehension and writing
- You do not need to judge writing quality by feel — the rubric tells you what Band a response falls in
- For comprehension: check that RACE was used; check that quotes are present; check that the answer is in full sentences

### Role 3: The Conversation Partner (Oral)
- Ask questions from the list in the Oral_Communication_Guide
- Push for extended answers: after the student answers, ask "Can you tell me more?" or "Why do you think that?"
- You do not need to correct every mistake — note errors and discuss them after

## Practical Tips for Harman

**Tip 1: Use the Error Log (Folder 03)**
After every practice session, fill in the error log. Over time, patterns will emerge — if the student keeps getting the same tense wrong, that's where to focus.

**Tip 2: Read Together**
Read a short newspaper article or magazine story together once a week. Discuss what it is about. Ask: "What is the main idea? What is one interesting thing you learned?" This builds comprehension naturally.

**Tip 3: Play "Improve This Sentence"**
Say a weak sentence aloud (e.g., "She was very scared and she ran away."). Ask your child to improve it using better vocabulary and techniques from the composition guide. This is quick and can be done anywhere — in the car, at dinner.

**Tip 4: Mark Compositions Together**
Read the composition aloud together. Identify the story arc: Where is the opening? Where is the climax? Does the ending leave an impression? Use the rubric to give a mark. Discuss one thing to improve next time.

**Tip 5: Celebrate Improvements**
Track scores over time in the Progress Tracker (Folder 03). Celebrate when the score improves, even by a small margin. Progress in language is slower than in Science or Maths — consistency is the key.

## Quick Wins (High Impact, Low Effort)

These actions give the best return on revision time:

| Action | Time | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| Learn RACE and apply it every single time | Once | High — adds marks to every open-ended question |
| Memorise 20 strong vocabulary words and use them in writing | 1 week | High — elevates Language band in compositions |
| Memorise correct format for formal letter and email | 30 min | High — secures 6 format marks in Situational Writing |
| Read the passage before answering comprehension questions | Habit | High — reduces misreading errors |
| Always address ALL bullet points in Situational Writing | Habit | High — prevents 3–5 mark deductions |
| Use a variety of sentence openers in composition | Ongoing | Medium-High — improves Organisation score |

## Exam Day Reminders

**Paper 4 (Oral) — July/August:**
- Arrive rested and calm
- Bring your school pass
- Speak clearly and slowly — don't rush
- If you forget a word, say "what I mean is..." and explain it another way

**Paper 3 (Listening) — October:**
- Read questions during preview time
- Don't panic if you miss something — keep listening
- Never leave an answer blank

**Papers 1 and 2 — October:**
- Read all instructions before starting
- For Paper 1: Do Situational Writing FIRST (time discipline)
- For Paper 2: Move on if stuck — come back to hard questions
- Check for careless errors in the last 5 minutes
