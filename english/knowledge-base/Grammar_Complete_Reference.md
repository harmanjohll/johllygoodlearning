## 1. TENSES

### Overview Table

| Tense | Form | When to Use | Example |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|
| Simple Present | base verb / verb+s | Habits, facts, routines | She walks to school every day. |
| Simple Past | verb+ed / irregular | Completed past actions | He walked to school yesterday. |
| Simple Future | will + base verb | Future plans/predictions | She will walk to school tomorrow. |
| Present Continuous | am/is/are + verb+ing | Action happening NOW | He is walking to school now. |
| Past Continuous | was/were + verb+ing | Action in progress in past | They were walking when it rained. |
| Present Perfect | has/have + past participle | Past action with present result | She has lost her bag. |
| Past Perfect | had + past participle | Action before another past action | He had eaten before she arrived. |

### Tense Rules

## 2. SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT (SVA)

**Rule:** The verb must agree with its subject in number and person.

### Core Rules

| Subject | Verb Form | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I | am / have / base verb | I am happy. I go. |
| You | are / have / base verb | You are clever. |
| He / She / It / Singular noun | is / has / verb+s | He is tall. She runs fast. |
| We / They / Plural noun | are / have / base verb | They are friendly. Dogs bark. |

### Tricky SVA Situations

**1. Phrases between subject and verb — ignore them:**
- The box of chocolates [is/are] on the table. → IS (subject = "box", not "chocolates")
- The students in the classroom [was/were] quiet. → WERE (subject = "students")

**2. Collective nouns — usually singular in Singapore English:**
- The team is ready.
- The class was noisy.
- The family has arrived.

**3. "Each" and "every" — always singular:**
- Each student has a book.
- Every child was present.

**4. Either...or / Neither...nor — verb agrees with CLOSER subject:**
- Neither the teacher nor the students are ready.
- Either the boys or the girl is responsible.

**5. Indefinite pronouns — usually singular:**
- Singular: everyone, everyone, someone, anyone, no one, nobody, everybody, each, either, neither
- Everyone has a role to play.
- Nobody was hurt.

### Common SVA Errors

| Wrong | Correct | Rule |
|-------|---------|------|
| The group of students are going. | The group ... is going. | "group" is singular |
| Everyone have done it. | Everyone has done it. | "everyone" is singular |
| Neither she nor he are wrong. | Neither she nor he is wrong. | "he" is closer — singular |
| My friend and I is going. | My friend and I are going. | compound subject = plural |

## 3. ARTICLES: A, AN, THE

### Rules

| Article | Use | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| a | Before singular countable noun; consonant SOUND | a book, a university (u sounds like "you") |
| an | Before singular countable noun; vowel SOUND | an apple, an honest man (h is silent) |
| the | Specific/known noun; unique things; second mention | the sun, the book I mentioned |
| (none) | Plural nouns in general; uncountable nouns in general | Dogs are loyal. Water is important. |

### The vs No Article

| Use "the" | Use no article |
|-----------|---------------|
| The Nile River (specific name) | Rivers are beautiful (general) |
| The book on the table (specific book) | Books are useful (general) |
| The moon (unique) | Space is vast (uncountable) |
| The Prime Minister (unique role) | Ministers work hard (general) |

### Common Article Errors

| Wrong | Correct | Rule |
|-------|---------|------|
| She is a honest girl. | She is an honest girl. | "honest" starts with vowel sound |
| A sun is bright. | The sun is bright. | Unique noun = the |
| I love the nature. | I love nature. | Uncountable noun in general = no article |
| He plays a guitar. | He plays the guitar. | Instruments with "play" = the |

## 4. PRONOUNS

### Types of Pronouns

| Type | Examples | Use |
|------|---------|-----|
| Subject pronouns | I, you, he, she, it, we, they | Subject of verb |
| Object pronouns | me, you, him, her, it, us, them | Object of verb/preposition |
| Possessive adjectives | my, your, his, her, its, our, their | Before a noun |
| Possessive pronouns | mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs | Replace noun phrase |
| Reflexive pronouns | myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves | Refer back to subject |

### Common Pronoun Errors

| Wrong | Correct | Rule |
|-------|---------|------|
| Between you and I | Between you and me | After preposition, use object pronoun |
| Me and my friend went | My friend and I went | Subject position = I |
| The dog hurt it's paw | The dog hurt its paw | "its" = possession; "it's" = it is |
| Everyone should do their... | Everyone should do his or her... | "everyone" is singular (though "their" is widely accepted now) |

## 6. CONJUNCTIONS

### Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS)

| Conjunction | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| For | Reason (formal) | She stayed home, for she was ill. |
| And | Addition | He sang and she danced. |
| Nor | Negative addition | He didn't eat, nor did he drink. |
| But | Contrast | I tried, but I failed. |
| Or | Alternative | Come now or come later. |
| Yet | Contrast (stronger) | She studied, yet she failed. |
| So | Result | It rained, so we stayed inside. |

### Subordinating Conjunctions

| Category | Conjunctions | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Time | when, while, after, before, since, until, as soon as | She cried after she heard the news. |
| Reason | because, since, as | He was late because he missed the bus. |
| Condition | if, unless, provided that | I will go if the rain stops. |
| Contrast | although, even though, though, whereas | Although it was late, she kept working. |
| Purpose | so that, in order that | She studied hard so that she could pass. |
| Result | so...that, such...that | It was so hot that we stayed inside. |

### Correlative Conjunctions (Pairs)

| Pair | Example |
|------|---------|
| both...and | Both she and he attended. |
| either...or | Either come early or don't come at all. |
| neither...nor | Neither the cat nor the dog ate the food. |
| not only...but also | She is not only smart but also hardworking. |
| whether...or | I don't know whether to go or stay. |

## 7. PUNCTUATION

### Full Stop, Question Mark, Exclamation Mark

| Mark | Use |
|------|-----|
| . | End of statement |
| ? | End of direct question |
| ! | Strong emotion or command |

### Comma

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| List of 3+ items | She bought apples, oranges, and bananas. |
| Before coordinating conjunction joining two main clauses | He was tired, but he continued. |
| After introductory phrase | After the rain, the sun appeared. |
| Around non-essential information | My teacher, who is very kind, helped me. |
| Direct address | "Come here, John." |

### Apostrophe

| Use | Example | Rule |
|-----|---------|------|
| Contraction | don't, can't, it's | Replaces missing letters |
| Singular possession | the boy's bag | Add 's |
| Plural possession (ends in s) | the boys' bags | Add ' only |
| Plural possession (not ending in s) | the children's toys | Add 's |

> NEVER use apostrophe for plural: "apple's" (wrong) vs "apples" (correct)

### Speech Marks (Quotation Marks)

**Rules for direct speech:**
1. Put speech marks around the EXACT words spoken: She said, "I am tired."
2. Start speech with a capital letter: She said, "Come here."
3. Punctuation goes INSIDE the closing speech mark: "Come here," she said.
4. New speaker = new paragraph

**Direct vs Indirect Speech:**

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| She said, "I am hungry." | She said (that) she was hungry. |
| He asked, "Are you ready?" | He asked if she was ready. |
| "Come here!" she ordered. | She ordered him to come there. |

**Key changes in indirect speech:**
- "I" → she/he
- "am" → was
- "here" → there
- "today" → that day
- "tomorrow" → the next day/the following day
- "yesterday" → the day before / the previous day

### Colon and Semicolon

| Mark | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| : | Introduce a list; introduce an explanation | She needed three things: a pencil, a ruler, and an eraser. |
| ; | Join two related main clauses; separate complex list items | She was tired; however, she continued working. |

## 8. ACTIVE VS PASSIVE VOICE

### What Is Active and Passive?

**Active:** Subject DOES the action → Subject + verb + object
**Passive:** Subject RECEIVES the action → Subject + to be + past participle (+ by + agent)

| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| The dog bit the boy. | The boy was bitten by the dog. |
| The teacher marked the papers. | The papers were marked by the teacher. |
| Someone stole my wallet. | My wallet was stolen. |
| They will announce the results tomorrow. | The results will be announced tomorrow. |

### Formula for Converting Active to Passive

**Simple Past:** was/were + past participle
**Simple Present:** am/is/are + past participle
**Simple Future:** will be + past participle
**Present Perfect:** has/have been + past participle
**Past Continuous:** was/were being + past participle

### When to Use Passive

- When the doer is unknown: "My bag was stolen."
- When the doer is unimportant: "The school was built in 1950."
- Formal/scientific writing: "The experiment was conducted over three days."

## 9. DIRECT AND INDIRECT SPEECH (Revision)

### Converting Direct to Indirect

**Statement:**
- Direct: He said, "I like chocolate."
- Indirect: He said (that) he liked chocolate.

**Question:**
- Direct: She asked, "Where do you live?"
- Indirect: She asked where he lived. (no question mark; no inversion)

**Yes/No Question:**
- Direct: He asked, "Are you ready?"
- Indirect: He asked if/whether she was ready.

**Command:**
- Direct: The teacher said, "Sit down."
- Indirect: The teacher told them to sit down.

### Tense Backshift Table

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|-----------------|
| am/is | was |
| are | were |
| was/were | had been |
| will | would |
| can | could |
| may | might |
| have/has | had |
| Simple present | Simple past |
| Simple past | Past perfect |
| Present continuous | Past continuous |

## 11. ADJECTIVES AND ADVERBS

### Adjectives
Describe nouns. Come before the noun or after linking verbs (be, seem, feel, look, sound, smell, taste).

- The tall boy ran.
- She felt happy.
- The food smells delicious.

### Adverbs
Describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. Many end in -ly.

- He ran quickly. (describes verb)
- She is very tall. (describes adjective)
- He spoke quite softly. (describes adverb)

### -ed vs -ing Adjectives (Common Errors)

| -ing (causes the feeling) | -ed (has the feeling) |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| The film is boring. | I am bored. |
| The news is shocking. | She is shocked. |
| The task is confusing. | He is confused. |
| The result is surprising. | They are surprised. |
| The lesson is interesting. | The student is interested. |

## 12. COMPARATIVES AND SUPERLATIVES

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| tall | taller | tallest |
| short | shorter | shortest |
| happy | happier | happiest |
| beautiful | more beautiful | most beautiful |
| good | better | best |
| bad | worse | worst |
| far | farther/further | farthest/furthest |
| little | less | least |
| many/much | more | most |

**Rules:**
- 1-2 syllable adjectives: add -er/-est
- 3+ syllable adjectives: use more/most
- Never use double: "more taller" (WRONG), "most happiest" (WRONG)
