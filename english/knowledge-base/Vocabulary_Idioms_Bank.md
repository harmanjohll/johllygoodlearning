# Vocabulary and Idioms Bank
### PSLE English Language — Primary 6

---

## How to Use This Bank

- **Weekly habit:** Learn 5 new words or idioms per week
- **Active learning:** Use new words in a sentence of your own
- **Writing:** Aim to use 2–3 words from this bank in every composition
- **Composition uplift:** Replace weak words with strong alternatives before submitting

# SECTION 5: TRANSITION WORDS FOR WRITING

Use these to connect ideas and make your writing flow smoothly.

### Adding Information
- Furthermore, ...
- In addition, ...
- Moreover, ...
- Also, ...
- Not only... but also...
- Besides this, ...

### Showing Contrast
- However, ...
- On the other hand, ...
- Nevertheless, ...
- Despite this, ...
- Although...
- In contrast, ...

### Showing Cause/Reason
- Because...
- Since...
- As a result of...
- Due to...
- This was caused by...

### Showing Effect/Result
- Therefore, ...
- As a result, ...
- Consequently, ...
- This led to...
- Thus, ...

### Showing Time Sequence
- First, ... Then, ... Finally, ...
- Initially, ...
- Shortly afterwards, ...
- Meanwhile, ...
- Eventually, ...
- In the end, ...
- At last, ...

### Giving Examples
- For example, ...
- For instance, ...
- Such as...
- In particular, ...
- To illustrate, ...

### Concluding/Summarising
- In conclusion, ...
- To sum up, ...
- Overall, ...
- In summary, ...
- All in all, ...

# SECTION 6: SENSORY VOCABULARY (For Compositions)

Strong descriptive writing uses all five senses.

### Visual (Sight)
gleaming, shimmering, looming, blazing, shadowy, vivid, glistening, towering, hazy, dazzling

### Auditory (Sound)
thunderous, faint, deafening, hushed, piercing, melodic, rumbling, crackling, whistling, rustling

### Tactile (Touch/Feel)
rough, velvety, prickly, ice-cold, clammy, scorching, silky, jagged, numb, trembling

### Olfactory (Smell)
pungent, fragrant, musty, acrid, floral, earthy, stale, fresh, overpowering, faint

### Gustatory (Taste)
bitter, tangy, savoury, bland, mouthwatering, rich, sharp, sweet, sour, refreshing
