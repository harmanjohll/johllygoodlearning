## What is "register"?

Register is the level of formality you choose in your writing or speaking. It is decided by **three factors** (the P.A.C. of PACW / PACT):

| Factor | Asks… | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Why am I writing/speaking? | To complain, to invite, to thank, to inform, to persuade, to instruct, to reflect |
| **Audience** | Who is reading/listening? | Principal, friend, stranger, public, parent, examiner |
| **Context** | What is the situation? | Formal complaint, party invitation, school report, casual chat |

The right register is the one that matches all three. A formal complaint to the principal sounds wrong if it uses *Hi Mr Tan!* The same opener works perfectly for an informal email to a friend's parent.

### Why register matters at PSLE

- **Situational Writing** marks register as part of Language & Organisation. A formal letter that uses contractions, slang, or emoji-style punctuation loses marks immediately.
- **Continuous Writing** is narrative, so register varies *inside* the story — narration is more formal; dialogue is more natural.
- **Oral SBC** rewards a mid-formal register (full sentences, no Singlish, no slang) regardless of theme.

## §1 — The three-tier register scale

| Tier | Used for | Markers |
|---|---|---|
| **Formal** | Letters to authority, official reports, formal speeches, formal articles, complaints | Full forms (do not), polite phrases (Could you kindly…), no slang, full names + titles, third person where possible |
| **Neutral** | Notices, informational reports, magazine articles, factual speeches | Mixed — formal vocabulary, but first person allowed, contractions sometimes acceptable |
| **Informal** | Emails to friends, casual recounts, diary entries, friendly reviews | Contractions (don't, can't), first names only, conversational phrases, exclamation marks for emphasis |

---

## §2 — Markers by tier

### Formal markers

- Salutations: *Dear Mr / Mrs / Ms [Surname], Dear Sir / Madam, Dear Principal Tan*
- Sign-offs: *Yours sincerely, Yours faithfully, With kind regards, Respectfully*
- Phrases: *I am writing to express / to enquire / to request / to inform you that…*
- Verbs: *enquire, request, inform, attend, assist, address, regret, appreciate, endeavour, propose, recommend*
- Adjectives: *sincere, appropriate, considerable, exemplary, prudent, courteous*
- No contractions: *do not, cannot, will not, would not, has not*
- No slang or idioms unless they aid clarity
- No exclamation marks (one closing line is the maximum)
- No emojis or text-speak (lol, btw, omw)

### Neutral markers

- Salutations: *Dear Mr Tan, Hi Mr Tan,* (depending on prior relationship)
- Sign-offs: *Best regards, Kind regards, Best wishes*
- Phrases: *I wanted to let you know that…, This is to inform you that…*
- Verbs: a mix of formal and conversational — *find, get, share, look at, deal with*
- Contractions: sparingly (*I'm, we're, that's*)
- Exclamation marks: one is fine

### Informal markers

- Salutations: *Hi [first name]!, Hey [first name],*
- Sign-offs: *Take care, See you soon, With love, Cheers, All the best*
- Phrases: *Guess what?, You won't believe what happened, How have you been?*
- Verbs: *get, grab, hang out, catch up, drop by, pop in*
- Contractions: free use (*I'm, I'll, can't, won't, isn't*)
- Conversational fillers: *anyway, by the way, oh, well, so*
- Exclamation marks: more freely (no more than 2–3 in a 150-word piece)

## §3 — Register matrix by Situational Writing genre

Every PSLE Situational Writing genre has a default register. This is the single most useful table in this KB.

| Genre | Default register | Audience | Tone marker | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Formal letter** | Formal | Authority (principal, manager, council) | Polite, restrained | No contractions; *Yours sincerely* if name known, *Yours faithfully* if "Sir/Madam" |
| **Formal email** | Formal | Authority, unfamiliar adult | Polite, professional | Subject line required; signature with role |
| **Informal letter / email** | Informal | Friend, relative, peer | Warm, conversational | Contractions allowed; first names; questions to engage |
| **Report** | Formal–neutral | Teacher, principal, council, news editor | Objective, factual | Title; section headings; numbered points; no "I" if possible (use "the writer", "this report") |
| **Speech** | Mid-formal to formal | Schoolmates, parents, public | Engaging, persuasive | First person allowed; rhetorical questions OK; vary sentence length for rhythm; address the audience directly ("Good morning everyone…") |
| **Article** (new in 2025) | Mid-formal | Magazine/newspaper readers (broad public) | Engaging, balanced, persuasive | Catchy headline; byline; opening hook; quotation marks for cited speech; first person sparingly; ends with reflective or call-to-action line |
| **Notice** | Formal-neutral | School community, public | Clear, concise | Title in bold/capitals; date; signature with title; no "I"; bullet points; key info first |
| **Review** | Informal-mid-formal | Magazine readers, peers | Balanced, opinionated | First person allowed; brief recommendation at end ("would highly recommend"); avoid extreme adjectives without justification |
| **Recount / Diary entry** | Informal | Self (diary) / friends (recount) | Personal, reflective | First person; past tense; sensory detail; can include feelings |

## §5 — Formal openers (situational writing)

Memorise five. Use one per piece.

| Purpose | Opener |
|---|---|
| Complaint | *I am writing to express my disappointment with…* |
| Request | *I am writing to request your permission for…* |
| Enquiry | *I am writing to enquire about…* |
| Thanks | *I am writing to express our sincere gratitude for…* |
| Suggestion / proposal | *I am writing to propose / to suggest that…* |
| Apology | *I am writing to apologise for…* |
| Information | *I am writing to inform you that…* |
| Invitation | *I am writing to extend a cordial invitation to…* |

---

## §6 — Formal closers (situational writing)

Pair with the right opener.

| Tone | Closer | Sign-off |
|---|---|---|
| Polite, hopeful | *I would be most grateful for your kind consideration of this matter.* | Yours sincerely / faithfully |
| Action-seeking | *I look forward to your prompt reply / response.* | Yours sincerely / faithfully |
| Thankful | *Once again, thank you for your time and assistance.* | Yours sincerely |
| Closing on warmth | *I appreciate your understanding in this matter.* | Yours sincerely |
| Open offer | *Should you require further information, please do not hesitate to contact me.* | Yours sincerely |

## §7 — Informal openers and closers

| Opener | Closer |
|---|---|
| *Hi [Name]! Hope you are doing well.* | *Take care and write back soon!* |
| *How are you? It's been ages since we last met!* | *Looking forward to hearing from you soon.* |
| *Guess what just happened…* | *Catch up soon!* |
| *I have so much to tell you!* | *Miss you loads.* |
| *Sorry I haven't been in touch — life's been hectic!* | *Big hugs to your family from mine.* |

## §8 — Register traps that cost marks

| Trap | Why it costs marks | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Contractions in a formal letter | *don't, can't, won't* signal informality | Use full forms |
| Slang in a formal speech | *cool, awesome, super* signal a child's voice | Use *commendable, impressive, remarkable* |
| Singlish leakage | *lah, lor, leh, hor* are conversational only | Excise entirely from any written piece |
| Emojis or text-speak | *lol, omg, btw, :)* are never acceptable | Excise entirely |
| First name in a formal letter | *Dear John* is too informal for an authority figure | Use *Dear Mr [Surname]* |
| Calling the principal "you guys" | Plural collective familiar pronoun | Use *the school administration, you and your staff* |
| Mixing tenses informally | *I went home and I am tired now* | Decide on past or present and stay there |
| Exclamation overuse in formal piece | *Thank you!! It was great!!!* signals juvenile excitement | One or none in formal writing |
| Asking "Right?" or "OK?" in formal writing | Conversational fillers | Remove entirely |
| Using *got* for all senses | *I got a letter / I got tired / I got there* — colloquial | *I received / I became / I arrived* |

## §9 — Register self-check (30-second drill)

Before submitting any Situational Writing piece, run this mental checklist:

1. **Salutation matches sign-off?** *Dear Mr Tan / Yours sincerely* ✓ — *Dear Sir or Madam / Yours faithfully* ✓ — *Hi John / Take care* ✓. Mismatch costs marks.
2. **No contractions in formal pieces?** Search for *'t* and *'s*.
3. **No slang or Singlish?** Search for *lah, lor, cool, awesome, super, guys*.
4. **Verbs upgraded?** Check that *get, ask, tell, help, show* are replaced with formal equivalents if the piece is formal.
5. **Tone consistent?** No sudden jokes in a complaint; no sudden coldness in a thank-you.
6. **Closer matches purpose?** *I look forward to your reply* for an action-seeking letter; *Thank you for your time* for a closed one.

## §10 — Mini-models by genre

### Formal letter — opening sentence

> *I am writing to express my concern about the rising number of accidents at the pedestrian crossing outside our school.*

### Article — opening hook + headline

> **Why we should care about our community library**  
> *By Aisha Lim, P6 Sunrise Primary School*  
>  
> *Tucked between the hawker centre and the bus stop, our community library is one of the most quietly important spaces in our neighbourhood — and most of us walk past it every day without a second thought.*

### Speech — opening

> *Good morning, Mrs Tan, fellow teachers and friends. Today I want to talk about something that has been on my mind for the past few weeks: the way we use our recess time.*

### Report — opening

> **Report on the Annual Sports Day, 2026**  
> *Prepared by: Sports Committee, P6 Sunrise Primary*  
> *Date: 17 May 2026*  
>  
> *1. Purpose: This report summarises the events of the Annual Sports Day, highlights two notable achievements, and recommends three improvements for the next edition.*

### Informal email — opening

> *Hi Priya!*  
>  
> *Guess what — Mum finally said yes to the camping trip! I'm so excited. I had to promise I'd help with the packing AND bring back proper photos this time (not just selfies), but it's all worth it.*

### Recount / diary — opening

> *Saturday, 16 May 2026*  
>  
> *I don't think I've ever been this nervous before a competition. The hall was crowded, the air conditioner was hissing, and my hands wouldn't stay still.*

### Notice — full short example

> **NOTICE**  
> **Inter-Class Spelling Bee**  
>  
> *Date: 24 May 2026*  
> *Time: 2.30 pm – 4.00 pm*  
> *Venue: School Hall*  
>  
> *All P5 and P6 students are invited to participate. Please register at the General Office by 20 May. Refreshments will be provided.*  
>  
> *Mrs Devi Krishnan*  
> *English Department Coordinator*
