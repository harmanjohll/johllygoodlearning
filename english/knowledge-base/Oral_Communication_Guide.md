# Oral Communication Guide
### PSLE English Language — Paper 4

---

## Overview

Paper 4 is conducted during July/August, before the written exams. It is worth 30 marks.

| Component | Marks | Duration |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Reading Aloud | 10 | ~2 minutes |
| Stimulus-Based Conversation | 20 | ~5–7 minutes |
| **Total** | **30** | ~10 minutes per student |

---

## COMPONENT 1: READING ALOUD (10 marks)

### What Happens
You receive a passage of approximately 150 words. You have about 10 minutes to prepare before reading it aloud to the examiner(s).

### What Is Assessed

| Criterion | Marks | What to Do |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | 4 | Say words correctly; stress the right syllable |
| Fluency & Pace | 3 | Read at a natural speed; do not rush or drag |
| Expression & Stress | 3 | Use your voice to convey meaning and emotion |

---

### Preparation Strategy (10 minutes)

**Minute 1–2: Read silently**
- Understand the content: What is this about? What is the mood?
- Mark punctuation: commas (pause), full stops (longer pause), exclamation marks (raise energy), question marks (raise voice slightly at end)

**Minute 3–5: Identify difficult words**
- Underline words you are not sure how to pronounce
- Sound them out: break into syllables
- If unsure, choose the pronunciation that sounds most natural and say it confidently

**Minute 6–8: Mark your passage**
- Put a "/" for slight pauses (comma)
- Put "//" for longer pauses (full stop, paragraph break)
- Underline key words to STRESS
- Mark dialogue: remind yourself to give characters different voices

**Minute 9–10: Practise in a whisper**
- Read through once, whispering, paying attention to pace

---

### Reading Aloud: Do and Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Read at a natural, comfortable pace | Rush through without pausing |
| Pause at punctuation marks | Ignore commas and full stops |
| Stress important words | Read in a flat, monotone voice |
| Show emotion in your voice for emotional parts | Sound mechanical or robotic |
| Look up from the page occasionally | Never look up (poor engagement) |
| Begin only when you are ready | Start immediately before you are settled |

### Common Pronunciation Challenges

| Word Type | Example | Common Error | Correct |
|-----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Silent letters | knight, wrap, knife | knight = "k-night" | "nite" |
| Unstressed syllables | comfortable | "com-for-ta-ble" | "CUMF-ter-bul" |
| -ed past tense | walked, stopped | "walk-ED" | "walkt", "stopt" |
| -ion endings | nation, situation | "nay-shun" natural | Maintain full pronunciation |
| Stress shift | reCORD (verb) vs REcord (noun) | Wrong stress | Note context |
| Common words | especially, particularly | "ex-specially" | "ess-PESH-al-ee" |

## COMPONENT 2: STIMULUS-BASED CONVERSATION (20 marks)

### What Happens
You are shown a picture or a short video clip. The examiner asks you questions about it. The conversation lasts about 5–7 minutes.

### What Is Assessed

| Criterion | Marks | What Examiners Look For |
|-----------|-------|------------------------|
| Communication of Ideas | 8 | Relevant, developed responses; opinions with reasons |
| Vocabulary | 6 | Range and accuracy of words used |
| Language Accuracy | 6 | Grammar, sentence structure, tense |

---

### The PEEL Framework for Every Response

Give structured, extended answers using PEEL:

| Letter | Meaning | What to Say |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| P | Point | State your main point/opinion clearly |
| E | Elaborate | Explain your point in more detail |
| E | Example | Give a specific example (from the picture OR from real life) |
| L | Link | Link back to the question or add a broader connection |

**Example:**
Question: "Do you think young people today spend too much time on their mobile phones?"

Weak answer: "Yes, I think so."

PEEL answer: "Yes, I believe many young people today spend too much time on their mobile phones. This is because smartphones offer constant entertainment and social connection, making it very easy to lose track of time. For example, I have friends who spend hours scrolling through videos online even when there are other tasks to complete. This concerns me because excessive phone use can affect both academic performance and physical health."

---

### How to Read a Stimulus Picture

When you see the picture, spend 30 seconds observing:

1. **Who is in the picture?** (People, age group, expressions)
2. **Where is the setting?** (Indoor/outdoor, specific place)
3. **What is happening?** (Main action or activity)
4. **What mood/atmosphere does it convey?** (Happy, tense, busy, peaceful)
5. **What issues or themes does it relate to?** (Technology, environment, community, health)

---

### Common Oral Themes and Vocabulary

#### Theme 1: Technology and Screen Time
Key vocabulary: social media, screen time, addicted, distracted, connected, isolated, cyberbullying, digital literacy, moderate, balance

Model sentence: "While technology has made communication more convenient, excessive use of social media can lead to social isolation among young people."

#### Theme 2: Environment and Sustainability
Key vocabulary: climate change, reduce, reuse, recycle, carbon footprint, renewable energy, conservation, deforestation, sustainable, environmentally-friendly

Model sentence: "I believe that individuals can make a significant difference by making small, everyday changes such as bringing reusable bags to the supermarket."

#### Theme 3: Community and Kindness
Key vocabulary: volunteer, empathy, compassion, inclusive, unity, community spirit, underprivileged, contribute, civic-minded, neighbourliness

Model sentence: "Acts of kindness, no matter how small, can have a profound impact on those who are struggling."

#### Theme 4: Education and Learning
Key vocabulary: holistic, critical thinking, creativity, collaboration, lifelong learning, enrichment, extracurricular, academic pressure, resilience

Model sentence: "I think a good education goes beyond textbooks — it also involves developing character and resilience."

#### Theme 5: Health and Lifestyle
Key vocabulary: well-being, mental health, physical activity, sedentary, balanced diet, stress, exercise, recreation, nutrition, obesity

Model sentence: "In my opinion, making time for physical activity is essential for both physical and mental well-being, especially for students who are under academic pressure."

#### Theme 6: Family and Relationships
Key vocabulary: bonding, communication, quality time, generation gap, mutual respect, responsibility, appreciation, support, trust

Model sentence: "I believe that open communication between parents and children helps build a relationship based on mutual trust and understanding."

---

### Extending Your Answers: Useful Phrases

**To give an opinion:**
- "I think/believe that..."
- "In my opinion..."
- "From my perspective..."
- "As far as I am concerned..."

**To elaborate:**
- "This is because..."
- "The reason for this is..."
- "To elaborate on my point..."

**To give an example:**
- "For instance..."
- "For example..."
- "A good example of this is..."
- "This is similar to what I have experienced..."

**To consider another view:**
- "On the other hand..."
- "Some people might argue that..."
- "While I understand that view, I feel that..."

**To link/conclude:**
- "Therefore, I believe..."
- "All in all..."
- "This is why I feel that..."

---

### When You Don't Know the Answer

If you don't understand the question:
- "I'm sorry, could you repeat the question please?"
- "Do you mean...?" (check your understanding)

If you need time to think:
- "That is an interesting question. Let me think for a moment..."
- "I have not thought about it in that way before, but I think..."

Never say nothing. Always attempt a response.

---

### Oral Practice Routine (For Harman)

**Daily: 5 minutes**
1. Show the student a picture (from newspaper, online, or a photo)
2. Ask: "What do you see? What do you think about this?"
3. Listen for: full sentences, at least 3–4 sentences per response, reasons given
4. Give one piece of feedback: "Try to use a different word instead of 'nice'."

**Weekly: 15 minutes**
1. Choose a theme from the list above
2. Ask 3–4 questions on the theme
3. Student answers using PEEL
4. Harman plays devil's advocate: "But don't you think...?" — encourage extended responses

**Before the exam:**
- Practise reading aloud from a Singapore newspaper or a storybook (not a textbook)
- Record the student on a phone; play it back together — check for pace, clarity, and expression
