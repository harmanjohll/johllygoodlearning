# Visual Text Examples — Knowledge Base
### PSLE English Language — Paper 2 Booklet A (Visual Text Comprehension, 5 marks)

> **Status:** Authored 2026-05-13. Holds ≥6 Singaporean public visual texts described, annotated, and accompanied by sample MCQ items with model answers. **No copyrighted images are embedded;** descriptions and source URLs only. The owner can pair these descriptions with screenshots stored locally in `source/reference/` if useful.
>
> Visual Text in the 2025+ Paper 2 is 5 marks (MCQ) in Booklet A — not 10 marks open-ended. Each example below supplies 4 MCQ items so a tutor or app can drill the full 5-mark feel using just 1–2 examples per session.

---

## Why Singapore-specific visual texts

Visual text comprehension rewards familiarity with the **conventions and design choices of Singaporean public communication**: MOH/HPB health campaigns, NEA environment campaigns, LTA traffic-safety posters, SCDF emergency-preparedness notices, library and community-centre flyers, school assembly slides. These appear in commuting spaces every day and use a recognisable register (mid-formal, didactic, often with a clear "call to action"). Reading them critically is a transferable skill the PSLE rewards.

## §1 — HPB / MOH: "Eat-Sleep-Move" campaign poster

**Visual description.** A bright poster on the wall of a hawker centre. A cartoon plate divided into three coloured quadrants: one filled with vegetables, one with grains, one with protein. Above the plate, three icons: a knife and fork (Eat), a pillow (Sleep), a running shoe (Move). The headline reads, in large yellow letters: *"Small steps. Big difference."* Below: bullet points — *"Eat the rainbow. Sleep at least 9 hours. Move every day."* Bottom-right corner: the HPB and Ministry of Health logos.

**Source.** Health Promotion Board public campaigns, browseable via `https://www.hpb.gov.sg`.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** According to the poster, how many hours of sleep does the campaign recommend?
   - A. At least 7 hours
   - B. At least 8 hours
   - C. **At least 9 hours**
   - D. Exactly 9 hours
   
   *Model.* The poster says "Sleep at least 9 hours." Option C uses the same phrase. Option D ("exactly") changes the meaning — common distractor pattern.

2. **(Inferential)** Who is the most likely target audience of this poster?
   - A. Doctors and nurses
   - B. **Children and their parents**
   - C. Hospital patients
   - D. Adults working long hours in offices
   
   *Model.* The cartoon style, the "rainbow" food language, and the placement in a hawker centre (not a clinic) all point to families with children.

3. **(Language use)** Why does the poster use the phrase *"Small steps. Big difference."*?
   - A. To explain the steps in a recipe
   - B. To warn readers about a serious illness
   - C. **To suggest that small daily changes can lead to a big improvement in health**
   - D. To advertise a fitness gym
   
   *Model.* This is the "purpose of a phrase" type — the phrase is a piece of persuasion, not a literal instruction.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** What is the main purpose of the poster?
   - A. To inform readers about a new health policy
   - B. **To encourage readers to adopt healthier daily habits**
   - C. To warn readers about junk food
   - D. To advertise a particular brand of vegetables
   
   *Model.* The campaign is health-promotion, not policy-information; the headline and bullets all push behaviour change.

## §2 — NEA: "Bin It. Don't Litter." poster

**Visual description.** A photograph of a void deck with a row of three colour-coded recycling bins (blue, green, yellow). Above the bins, in white block letters on a red banner: *"BIN IT. DON'T LITTER."* Below the photo, three icons: a plastic bottle, a paper cup, and a banana peel, each with an arrow pointing to the correct bin. Small fine print at the bottom: *"Fines start at $300. Repeat offenders may be issued a Corrective Work Order."* NEA logo, bottom-right.

**Source.** National Environment Agency public-area signage; campaigns at `https://www.nea.gov.sg`.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** According to the poster, what is the minimum fine for littering?
   - A. $30
   - B. $300
   - C. $3,000
   - D. The fine is not stated.
   
   *Model.* "Fines start at $300" — option B. The "starts at" phrasing makes A and C wrong; D is wrong because the fine IS stated.

2. **(Inferential)** What can you infer about NEA's view of repeat litterers?
   - A. They are excused if they apologise.
   - B. They receive a warning letter only.
   - C. **They are treated more seriously than first-time offenders.**
   - D. They are publicly named.
   
   *Model.* "Repeat offenders may be issued a Corrective Work Order" — a stronger consequence than the base fine implies escalating seriousness.

3. **(Language use)** Why does the poster use BLOCK LETTERS for "BIN IT. DON'T LITTER."?
   - A. To save printing ink
   - B. **To attract attention and convey urgency**
   - C. Because the designer ran out of small letters
   - D. To make the poster easier for animals to see
   
   *Model.* Block letters in red are a deliberate visual choice to grab the eye and signal seriousness.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** What is the most likely audience of this poster?
   - A. NEA officers on duty
   - B. **The general public passing through the void deck**
   - C. School principals
   - D. Tourists arriving at the airport
   
   *Model.* Void-deck placement, sample items (plastic bottle, paper cup, banana peel) all indicate everyday residents.

## §3 — LTA / Singapore Road Safety Council: "See. Be Seen." pedestrian poster

**Visual description.** A photograph at dusk of a child wearing dark clothing crossing a zebra crossing. The child is faint in the dim light. Beside the child, a second figure wears a bright-yellow reflective sash and is clearly visible. Headline above: *"See. Be Seen."* Subline: *"Wear bright or reflective clothing when walking after dark."* Bottom strip: a row of small icons showing different reflective accessories (sashes, wristbands, shoe clips). Logos: LTA and the Singapore Road Safety Council.

**Source.** LTA public-information campaigns, browseable via `https://www.lta.gov.sg`.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** According to the poster, what should pedestrians wear when walking after dark?
   - A. Black clothing
   - B. Comfortable shoes
   - C. **Bright or reflective clothing**
   - D. Helmets
   
2. **(Inferential)** What can you infer is the main risk shown in the photograph?
   - A. The child might fall.
   - B. **A driver might not see the child in time.**
   - C. The child might get cold.
   - D. The child might lose his way.

3. **(Language use)** Why does the poster use the short phrase *"See. Be Seen."*?
   - A. Because the designer forgot the rest of the sentence
   - B. **Because two short, balanced phrases are easier to remember**
   - C. To rhyme with the names of the products shown
   - D. Because there was little space on the poster

4. **(Purpose / audience)** What is the main purpose of this poster?
   - A. To advertise reflective clothing brands
   - B. **To persuade pedestrians, especially children, to be more visible at night**
   - C. To warn drivers to slow down
   - D. To remind people to use a torch at night

## §4 — SCDF: "What to do during a fire" infographic

**Visual description.** A vertical infographic split into four numbered steps. Step 1: a cartoon figure shouts "Fire!" — captioned *"Alert others immediately."* Step 2: the figure crouches low under smoke — captioned *"Stay low; smoke rises."* Step 3: the figure reaches a corridor with a fire exit sign — captioned *"Use the nearest staircase. NEVER use the lift."* Step 4: the figure stands outside a building waving at fire engines — captioned *"Call 995 once you are safe."* Header at the top: *"Every second counts."* SCDF logo, bottom.

**Source.** Singapore Civil Defence Force public-education materials at `https://www.scdf.gov.sg`.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** According to the infographic, what should you do BEFORE calling 995?
   - A. Open all the windows
   - B. Find a torchlight
   - C. **Make sure you are safe (outside the building)**
   - D. Take photos for evidence

2. **(Inferential)** What can you infer about why the infographic uses numbered steps?
   - A. The infographic has no other purpose.
   - B. **The order of actions is critical during an emergency.**
   - C. The steps reveal who designed the poster.
   - D. The numbering is for decoration only.

3. **(Language use)** Why is the word "NEVER" written in capital letters?
   - A. The designer made a typo.
   - B. **To stress an absolute rule that must not be broken.**
   - C. To balance the rest of the poster visually.
   - D. To save space.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** The infographic is most likely placed in which of these locations?
   - A. Inside a fire engine
   - B. **Inside the lift lobby of a public housing block**
   - C. Inside a swimming pool changing room
   - D. Inside a school library

## §5 — Public library: "Borrow a Book, Borrow a World" promotional flyer

**Visual description.** A landscape flyer with a photograph of a child seated cross-legged on the floor of a library, an open book in her lap, looking up wide-eyed. Around her, the photo has been edited so that book covers float in the air like islands. Headline (large): *"Borrow a book. Borrow a world."* Subline: *"Eight free borrows. Unlimited possibilities."* Bottom strip: opening hours, a QR code for the library app, and the NLB (National Library Board) logo.

**Source.** NLB campaigns at `https://www.nlb.gov.sg`.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** According to the flyer, how many books can a member borrow for free?
   - A. Two
   - B. Five
   - C. **Eight**
   - D. Unlimited

2. **(Inferential)** Why is the photograph edited to show book covers floating in the air?
   - A. To indicate that books are stored on high shelves
   - B. **To suggest that books open up new worlds and possibilities**
   - C. To make readers think the books are flying away
   - D. To advertise a new edition of the books

3. **(Language use)** Why does the headline pair "Borrow a book" with "Borrow a world"?
   - A. The library does not have many books left.
   - B. **To suggest that borrowing a book leads to a much bigger experience.**
   - C. The library is also selling globes.
   - D. To match the title of a popular movie.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** Who is the flyer most likely targeting?
   - A. Adult librarians
   - B. Tourists at the airport
   - C. **Families with school-going children**
   - D. Book sellers

## §6 — School-level: "Cyber Wellness Week" assembly slide

**Visual description.** A bright slide projected at a primary-school assembly. The headline reads: *"This week is Cyber Wellness Week."* In the centre, three circles overlap (Venn-diagram style): "Safe", "Kind", "Smart" online. Each circle has 2–3 bullet points: *Safe* says "Strong password / Private profile / Tell a trusted adult". *Kind* says "Think before you post / Stand up to cyberbullying." *Smart* says "Check sources / Spot fake news." Bottom-right: the school logo and the MOE Cyber Wellness logo.

**Source.** MOE Cyber Wellness curriculum materials (publicly available via `https://www.moe.gov.sg`).

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** Which three values does the slide focus on for online behaviour?
   - A. Quick, Quiet, Quality
   - B. **Safe, Kind, Smart**
   - C. Strong, Steady, Successful
   - D. Read, Reply, Repost

2. **(Inferential)** Why are the three circles drawn overlapping rather than separated?
   - A. The designer made a mistake.
   - B. **To suggest that the three values are related and should be practised together.**
   - C. To save space on the slide.
   - D. To match a school logo design.

3. **(Language use)** Why is "Tell a trusted adult" included as part of being **Safe**?
   - A. To make the bullet list longer.
   - B. **Because asking for adult help is part of staying safe when something goes wrong online.**
   - C. Because trusted adults read all online posts.
   - D. To remind students who their trusted adults are.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** What is the most likely purpose of this slide?
   - A. To advertise a new online game
   - B. To welcome new students
   - C. **To teach primary-school students how to behave online**
   - D. To select students for a Cyber Wellness Club

## §7 — Bonus example: bus-stop advertisement for an art exhibition

**Visual description.** A large bus-stop advertisement showing a swirl of bright watercolour paint forming the silhouette of a flying bird. Across the top in elegant black serif type: *"WINGS — A Children's Art Exhibition."* Below: *"Free Admission. 5–25 June 2026, National Gallery."* The fine print at the bottom: *"In partnership with the Singapore Children's Society. Proceeds support children's mental wellness programmes."* The Singapore Children's Society logo and the National Gallery logo are on the bottom-right.

**Sample MCQs.**

1. **(Literal)** When does the exhibition run?
   - A. 5–15 May 2026
   - B. **5–25 June 2026**
   - C. The full month of June 2026
   - D. The whole of 2026

2. **(Inferential)** What can you infer is the cause supported by this exhibition?
   - A. Wildlife conservation
   - B. **Children's mental wellness**
   - C. Singapore arts education
   - D. Free admission for tourists

3. **(Language use)** Why is the title *"WINGS"* written in large block capitals?
   - A. The designer used the only typeface available.
   - B. **To draw attention from a distance, especially from passers-by at bus stops.**
   - C. To match the watercolour bird image visually.
   - D. To save printing costs.

4. **(Purpose / audience)** Who is the main audience of this advertisement?
   - A. Professional artists only
   - B. Tourists in Singapore for the weekend
   - C. **The general public, especially families with children**
   - D. Bus drivers

## §8 — How to author a new visual-text MCQ set (for future expansion)

For each new visual text the owner adds, follow this template:

1. **Describe the visual in 3–6 sentences.** Include layout, dominant colours, headline, sub-line, fine print, logos, and any image content.
2. **Source URL.**
3. **MCQ 1 (Literal)** — test a specific piece of stated information. Two distractors must change one detail (a number, a date, a name).
4. **MCQ 2 (Inferential)** — test something implied but not stated (audience, purpose-related, cause).
5. **MCQ 3 (Language use)** — test why a word, phrase, font, or layout choice was made.
6. **MCQ 4 (Purpose / audience)** — test the overall function of the visual.
7. **Model answers and explanations** for each.
