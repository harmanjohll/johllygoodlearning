# PSLE English AL1 Master Knowledge Base (2025/2026)

## Part 1: Continuous Writing (Paper 1)
Top-band AL1 compositions score in the 33–36 mark range and require psychologically real characters, sensory details, tense climaxes, and reflective endings [1].

### The Story Mountain Blueprint
*   **Introduction (C.O.R.E.):** Catch attention (start in the present or *in medias res*), Object Anchor, Root Cause, and Establish Flashback [2-4]. 
*   **Rising Action (S.T.E.P.):** Suspense & Proportion (don't rush the crux), Two Conflicts (internal/external), Elaborate on the action, and use a Physical Action-List (3-4 steps) [5, 6].
*   **Climax (P.E.A.K.):** Physical reactions (using F.A.S.T.), Emotions, Action, and a Key Realisation [7].
*   **Falling Action (E.A.S.E.):** Ease tension, Account for characters, Solve & Show Consequences, and Evolution (no generic morals) [8, 9].
*   **Conclusion (R.E.F.):** Reflection tied to the Object Anchor, Emotions, and Future action [8-10]. Avoid banned phrases like "I learnt a valuable lesson" [11].

### Advanced Craft Techniques
*   **Show, Don't Tell (F.A.S.T.):** Describe the character's **F**ace, **A**ctions, **S**peech, and **T**houghts instead of stating emotions directly [12-14].
*   **Weather as Mood:** Use weather to mirror emotions, such as dark clouds for guilt or golden sunlight for hope [15].
*   **Sentence Rhythm:** Use short sentences to build tension and long sentences for immersion and atmosphere [16-18].

### The Reader's Lens (Strict Mechanics)
*   **Zero Tolerance for Sudden Appearances:** Events, objects, or consequences must have a clearly established root cause [6].
*   **Onomatopoeia:** Sound words (e.g., *Piak!*, *Thwack!*) do not require inverted commas [6, 19].
*   **Numbers:** Numbers under ten must be spelled out (e.g., first, fifth) to avoid spelling penalties [6].

## Part 2: High-Impact Vocabulary Bank
*"The difference between the right word and the almost-right word is the difference between lightning and the lightning bug." — Mark Twain* [20].

### Precision Emotions
*   **Fear / Nervousness:** Unnerved, hollow with dread, rigid, sick with fear [21].
*   **Sadness / Guilt:** Bereft, hollow, deflated, desolate, wistful [21].
*   **Anger:** Seething, indignant, bristling, cold-furious [21].
*   **Happiness / Relief:** Elated, luminous, giddy, quietly content [21].
*   **Surprise:** Floored, disbelieving, stunned, wrong-footed [21].

### Thematic Expressions
*   **Overcoming Fear:** Scared stiff, bundle of nerves, butterflies in my stomach [22].
*   **A Celebration:** Like a dog with two tails, on cloud nine, in seventh heaven [23].
*   **An Unplanned Visit:** Bolt from the blue, caught off guard [24].
*   **A Heroic Act:** Nerves of steel, bite the bullet, face the music, rise to the occasion [25].
*   **Developing a Good Habit:** Blood, sweat and tears, kick the habit, make a fresh start [26].

### Words Worth Mastering
*   **Surreptitiously:** Doing something secretly [27].
*   **Inevitable:** Unable to be avoided [27].
*   **Euphoric:** Intensely happy [27].
*   **Paradox:** A seemingly contradictory statement that reveals a truth [27].
*   **Reticent:** Reluctant to reveal feelings [27].

## Part 3: Situational Writing (Paper 1)
Situational Writing requires writing with purpose for a specific audience and context [28]. 

### The P.A.C.W. Framework
Before writing, lock in these four dimensions:
1.  **Purpose:** What specific action do you want the reader to take? [29].
2.  **Audience:** Who is receiving this, and what are their hidden concerns/biases? [29].
3.  **Context:** What is the situation (formal, sensitive, celebratory)? [30].
4.  **Writer Persona:** What role are you adopting (e.g., student leader, concerned neighbor)? [30].

### The 10-Minute Formula (For 14/14 Marks)
1.  Identify PACW and underline the critical-thinking point [31, 32].
2.  Number all 6 content points in the stimulus [32].
3.  Determine format (Formal/Informal) [32].
4.  Write the piece, checking off each numbered point [32].
5.  Proofread for tone, tense, and spelling [32].

## Part 4: Oral Communication (Paper 4)
Oral Communication carries 40 marks (20% of the total PSLE grade) and is critical for achieving an AL1 [33, 34].

### Reading Aloud (R.E.A.P. Framework)
Read based on the context provided in the preamble [35].
*   **Rhythm:** Do not rush; pause at commas and full stops [36].
*   **Expression:** Convey emotion matching the preamble [36].
*   **Articulation:** Pronounce every syllable, especially "-ed", "-ing", and "-th" endings [36, 37].
*   **Pace:** Vary pace for excitement or importance [36].

### Stimulus-Based Conversation (SBC)
*   **Question 1 (Observation):** Describe the image and infer what it means [38-40].
*   **Question 2 (Personal Experience):** Use PEEL (Point, Evidence, Explain, Link) or OREO (Opinion, Reason, Example, Opinion) to share an authentic experience [40, 41].
*   **Question 3 (Critical Opinion):** Use the **Validate-Bridge-Persuade (VBP)** model. Acknowledge the opposing view genuinely, bridge to your perspective, and persuade with specific reasons [42, 43]. Always aim to answer the "hidden need" beneath the examiner's question [43, 44].
