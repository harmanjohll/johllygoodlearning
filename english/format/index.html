PSLE English Language Format

200 marks · 4 papers · about 4 hours 25 minutes total. Implemented from PSLE 2025 (SEAB Syllabus 0001).

2025 changes: Paper 1 dropped from 55m to 50m (Situational 14, Continuous 36; Article writing added as a genre). Paper 4 rose from 30m to 40m (Reading Aloud 15 with a new preamble, SBC 25 with real-life photo stimuli; the two parts are no longer thematically linked). Synthesis & Transformation is retained at 10m. Visual Text moved to Booklet A as a 5-mark MCQ.

Paper 1 · Writing

50 marks · 1 h 10 min · 25%
ComponentItemsMarks
Part 1 — Situational Writing1 functional piece (letter, email, report, speech, article, etc.)14
Part 2 — Continuous Writing1 narrative composition (≥150 words; 3 picture options)36

Paper 2 · Language Use & Comprehension

90 marks · 1 h 50 min · 45%
ComponentItemsMarks
Booklet A (25 marks · MCQ)
Grammar MCQ10 × 1m10
Vocabulary MCQ5 × 1m5
Vocabulary Cloze5 × 1m5
Visual Text Comprehension5 × 1m5
Booklet B (65 marks · open response)
Grammar Cloze10 × 1m10
Editing for Spelling and Grammar10 × 1m10
Comprehension Cloze15 × 1m15
Synthesis & Transformation5 × 2m10
Comprehension Open-Ended~10 questions on 1 passage (narrative or non-narrative, 500–600 words)20

Paper 3 · Listening Comprehension

20 marks · ~35 min · 10%
ComponentItemsMarks
MCQ on spoken texts (read twice)20 × 1m20

Audio text types: recount, entertain, instruct, describe, inform, explain, respond, argue, evaluate, persuade. First 7 items have graphic representations.

Paper 4 · Oral Communication

40 marks · ~10 min + 5 min prep · 20%
ComponentItemsMarks
Reading Aloud (new preamble; PACT + REAP)1 passage15
Stimulus-Based Conversation (real-life photo)1 photo · ~5 min25

The two parts are not thematically linked in the 2025+ format.

Total

200 marks · 100%
