Key Questions
Glossary

🦎 What is an adaptation?

An adaptation is a feature or behaviour that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its habitat. Every part of an organism's body, and every regular thing it does, has been shaped over many generations by the environment it lives in.

🔑 Adaptations are inherited. An individual does NOT grow an adaptation because it needs one; populations adapt over generations.

🧬 Structural vs behavioural

  • Structural adaptations are physical features of the body.
    Polar bear: thick fur, blubber, small ears. Cactus: spines, thick stem, deep roots. Duck: webbed feet, oily feathers.
  • Behavioural adaptations are actions the organism does.
    Birds migrate. Bears hibernate. Owls hunt at night. Herring swim in schools.

🌵 Same habitat → similar adaptations

Organisms that share a habitat often share similar features because the same environmental pressures favour the same solutions:

  • Desert: thick skin or waxy coat to reduce water loss; active at night; store water.
  • Polar: thick fur or blubber; white colouring; compact body shape to reduce heat loss.
  • Rainforest: camouflage; sharp senses; climbing or gliding adaptations.
  • Ocean: streamlined body, gills, fins, salt glands.

⏳ When habitats change

Adaptation takes generations. If a habitat changes faster than the population can adapt — a forest cut down, a climate warming too quickly, a river polluted — the species may shrink in number or go extinct. This is why habitat protection matters.

Summary

An adaptation is an inherited feature or behaviour that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its habitat. Structural adaptations are body features; behavioural adaptations are actions. Organisms in the same habitat often share similar adaptations. When habitats change faster than populations can adapt, species may decline or become extinct.

What to notice

Pay attention to the pattern: in each habitat, different animals converge on similar solutions (thick insulation in cold places; water conservation in deserts; streamlining underwater). That is the footprint of adaptation over generations.
