🎵 How Sound is Produced

Sound is produced when objects VIBRATE (move rapidly back and forth).

  • Guitar string vibrates → sound
  • Vocal cords vibrate → voice
  • Drum skin vibrates → drumbeat

When you pluck a guitar string, it vibrates rapidly → pushes and pulls the air around it → creates compressions (particles pushed together) and rarefactions (particles spread apart) → these travel outward as sound waves.

🔑 When vibrations stop → sound stops. No vibration = no sound.

📡 How Sound Travels

Sound needs a MEDIUM (material) to travel through — it is a mechanical wave.

  • Can travel through: solids (fastest), liquids (medium speed), gases (slowest)
  • Cannot travel through a VACUUM (empty space with no particles)
  • Why: Sound waves need particles to compress and rarefy. A vacuum has no particles.
  • Example: On the Moon (vacuum), astronauts cannot hear explosions — they must use radio.
Medium Speed of Sound
Solid (iron) ~5,000 m/s
Liquid (water) ~1,500 m/s
Gas (air) ~340 m/s

🎶 Pitch and Loudness

Pitch — how high or low a sound is:

  • High pitch = high frequency = rapid vibration (thin, tight, or short strings)
  • Low pitch = low frequency = slow vibration (thick, loose, or long strings)
  • Examples: woman's voice (high pitch), man's voice (lower pitch), bass guitar (low pitch)

Loudness — how loud or soft a sound is:

  • Loud sound = large amplitude = large vibration (lots of energy)
  • Soft sound = small amplitude = small vibration (little energy)
  • Striking a drum harder → larger vibration → louder sound
🎯 Pitch depends on FREQUENCY. Loudness depends on AMPLITUDE.

🏔 Echoes

An echo is a reflected sound — sound that bounces off a hard flat surface and reaches your ears again after the original sound.

  • Requires: a hard flat surface (cliff, building wall) and enough distance for the echo to be heard separately
  • Echoes are used in sonar (ships measuring ocean depth), ultrasound (medical imaging), and by bats for echolocation
Summary

Sound is produced by VIBRATION. It travels as waves through a medium (solid, liquid, gas) — FASTEST through solids. It CANNOT travel through a vacuum. PITCH depends on frequency (fast vibration = high pitch). LOUDNESS depends on amplitude (large vibration = loud). ECHOES are reflected sound waves that bounce off hard flat surfaces.
