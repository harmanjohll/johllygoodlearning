Glossary
Energy
The ability to do work. Measured in joules (J). Exists in many forms and can be converted from one form to another.
Kinetic energy
The energy of a moving object. A fast car has more kinetic energy than a slow one.
Potential energy
Stored energy due to an object's position or condition. Two main types: gravitational (due to height) and elastic (stretched or compressed).
Heat energy
A form of energy that flows from a hotter object to a cooler one. Usually the wasted output of energy converters.
Chemical energy
Energy stored in the bonds between atoms in food, fuel, and batteries. Released when bonds are broken.
Energy conversion
Changing energy from one form to another. No energy is destroyed; the useful output and wasted output together equal the input.
Wasted energy
The output energy form we do not want. Almost always includes heat, and sometimes sound. Wasted energy is not lost; it spreads into the surroundings.
Law of conservation of energy
Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be converted. Total energy before = total energy after.

🔋 What is Energy?

Energy is the ability to do work. It exists in many forms, and every form can be converted into every other form.

  • Kinetic energy — the energy of motion (a running child, a falling ball).
  • Potential energy — stored energy. Gravitational (due to height) or elastic (stretched or compressed).
  • Heat energy — flows from hotter to cooler objects.
  • Light energy — lets us see; given off by the Sun, flames, bulbs.
  • Sound energy — produced by vibrating objects.
  • Electrical energy — carried by moving electric charges.
  • Chemical energy — stored in bonds between atoms in food, fuel, and batteries.
🔑 Energy is measured in joules (J). A single Smartie has about 20,000 J of chemical energy.

🔁 Energy Conversion

An energy converter is a device that changes one form of energy into another. Every everyday device is an energy converter:

  • A bulb: electrical energy → light energy + heat energy
  • A fan / motor: electrical energy → kinetic energy + heat energy + sound energy
  • A battery: chemical energy → electrical energy
  • A solar cell: light energy → electrical energy
  • A generator / dynamo: kinetic energy → electrical energy
  • A plant leaf (photosynthesis): light energy → chemical energy
  • Your muscles: chemical energy → kinetic energy + heat energy
  • A loudspeaker: electrical energy → sound energy + heat energy

♻️ The Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be converted from one form into another. The total amount of energy before a conversion equals the total amount after.

Input energy = Useful output energy + Wasted output energy

Wasted energy is not lost — it just spreads out into the surroundings, usually as heat and sometimes as sound. That is why a bulb that has been on for a while feels warm, and a running motor hums.

🌞 The Sun as Earth's Energy Source

Almost all the energy we use traces back to the Sun. Plants convert light energy into chemical energy (photosynthesis). Animals eat plants and other animals to get that energy. Coal, oil, and gas are the remains of ancient plants and animals, so their chemical energy also came originally from the Sun.

🌱 Renewable vs Non-Renewable

  • Renewable: solar, wind, moving water (hydro), geothermal, biomass. Can be replaced naturally within a short time.
  • Non-renewable: coal, oil, natural gas, nuclear fuel. Take millions of years to form; burning them releases carbon dioxide and contributes to climate change.
Summary

Energy is the ability to do work. It exists in many forms — kinetic, potential, heat, light, sound, electrical, chemical — and can be converted between them. Every device is an energy converter: its input energy becomes useful output energy plus some wasted energy, usually in the form of heat. By the law of conservation of energy, the total never changes; wasted energy is not destroyed, just spread out. The Sun is Earth's main source of energy.

What to notice

Every device produces at least two outputs. The useful output is the form we want. The wasted output is usually heat, and sometimes sound. By the law of conservation of energy, useful + wasted always equals the input.
